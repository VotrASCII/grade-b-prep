01

Polity & Governance

  • President’s visit to Sikkim (26‑28 May 2026) – The President inspected the Namgyal Institute of Tibetology, attended the Nathu La Pass convocation and presented the President’s Police Colour to the Sikkim Police. The events underscored the centre‑state protocol that reinforces federalism and cultural diplomacy.
  • PM–CM meeting (25 May 2026) – Chief Minister Mohan Charan Majhi met PM Narendra Modi, who posted a Sanskrit Subhashitam on X. The interaction illustrated the Ministerial outreach protocols and the use of social media for policy communication.
  • Parliamentary Consultative Committee of AYUSH (25 May 2026) – Chair Prataprao Jadhav emphasized yoga and naturopathy, citing > 70 BNYS colleges and 700,000+ certified professionals. This highlights the central government's focus on traditional medicine as a cultural policy.
  • Elections Commission Workshop (25–29 May 2026) – 32 participants from 12 countries attended a two‑week risk‑management workshop, evidencing India’s expanding role in global electoral standards and reinforcing the constitutional mandate for free and fair elections.
  • Central Sector Infrastructure Flash Report (25 May 2026) – 1,981 projects worth ₹42.78 lakh crore were monitored; 801 projects had > 80 % physical progress. The report furnishes data critical for policy assessments and transparent public‑sector governance.
  • NITI Aayog’s semiconductor roadmap (29 May 2026) – The roadmap targets a USD 120‑150 billion value chain by 2035. It reflects India’s ambition to become a global semiconductor hub, aligning with the national science & technology policy.
  • High‑Level National Highway Review (28 May 2026) – Nitin Gadkari reviewed 10,064 km of NHs in Rajasthan and 1,947 km in Himachal Pradesh, highlighting infrastructure priorities and the hub‑and‑spoke model for regional connectivity.
  • Cabinet Committee on Demographic Change (26 May 2026) – Justice Prakash Prabhakar Navlekar chaired a committee on illegal immigration, signalling a constitutional drive to monitor demographic shifts that affect social security and national security.
02

Economy & Finance

  • RBI’s Treasury Bills auction (27 May 2026) – 91‑, 182‑, and 364‑day TBs were auctioned with 20,104.36 crore, 19,900.25 crore, and 8,815 crore face values respectively. Results show India’s debt‑management strategy and monetary‑policy signals.
  • Government of India Dated Securities auction (29 May 2026) – Two debt instruments (6.68 % GS 2040 & 7.43 % GS 2076) totaling ₹28,000 crore were issued, with a 17,000 crore and 11,000 crore allotment respectively. The sale platform illustrates fiscal policy normalisation and investor confidence.
  • State Government Securities auction (26 May 2026) – Assam, Bihar, Chhattisgarh, Gujarat, Kerala and Madhya Pradesh issued SGS up to ₹1,260 crore, with 9,300 crore of the amount kept for fiscal financing. The results contribute to public finance sustainability.
  • RBI’s Variable‑Rate Repo auction (29 May 2026) – 3‑day VRR was conducted at a cut‑off rate of 5.26 %, reflecting the liquidity environment and the RBI’s policy toolkit.
  • Bank Credit Statistics (31 May 2026) – Scheduled commercial banks recorded ₹192 lakh crore credit growth: 12.3 % overall and 8 % in agriculture. The data inform policy on credit allocation and economic growth.
  • Digital Payments Growth (26 May 2026) – Total broadband subscribers rose to 1,073.44 M; mobile‑FWA to 1,268.96 M; active mobile subscribers to 1,193.57 M. This underscores the shift to digital financial inclusion and the success of Jan Dhan and UPI ecosystems.
  • Sovereign Gold Bond redemption price (29 May 2026) – The redemption price for SGB 2021‑22 Series‑II was ₹15,672 per unit, calculated on the mean gold price of May 26‑29. This data is crucial for understanding the gold market and household investment patterns.
  • Rural Infrastructure – Power Transmission (25 May 2026) – A 20 GW power transmission project was unveiled at ₹688.74 cr, amplifying the central strategy to connect green hydrogen hubs and rural electrification.

International Relations

  • India‑Nordic Summit (19 May 2026, Oslo) – The third summit focused on green‑technology, digital innovation and maritime cooperation, launching a climate‑action partnership.
  • India‑Canada CEPA talks (26 May 2026) – The largest business delegation visited Canada; the objective was to conclude a CEPA worth USD 50 bn by 2030, reflecting trade liberalisation ambitions.
  • India‑Japan workforce seminar (25 May 2026) – About 250 Japanese representatives attended a seminar on skill recognition; the event aimed at boosting manufacturing and green‑economy collaboration.
  • BRICS Tourism Working Group chairship (25 May 2026) – India chaired the group with a theme of resilience and sustainability, setting guidelines for cooperation beyond the summit in August 2026.
  • India‑Korea CEPA (25–27 May 2026) – The 12th round of talks emphasised digital trade and strategic industrial ties, mirroring a broader South‑East Asian economic alignment.
  • India–Australia defence dialogue (31 May 2026) – The defence exchange highlighted interoperability, 5G maritime cooperation and Indo‑Pacific security architecture under the joint framework.
  • High‑Level India–Spain delegation (28 May 2026) – Jitendra Singh met Spanish leaders to advance astrophysics and circular‑economy cooperation, indicating the role of science diplomacy in bilateral relations.

Environment & Ecology

  • UNFCCC COP‑28 Climate Finance – India reiterated its commitment to the Paris Agreement, pledging a ₹1.5 trn net‑zero finance package for 2035, demonstrating policy continuity on climate change.
  • State‑level Air‑Pollution Action (26 May 2026) – Delhi‑NCR launched a new air‑pollution action plan targeting vehicle‑emissions and infrastructure upgrades.
  • Chei‑River Ecology Rehabilitation (24 May 2026) – National Biodiversity Authority approved a ₹530 cr project to revive the Chei‑River corridor, aligning with Ramsar site restoration mandates.
  • Protected Area Expansion – The Ministry of Environment announced a new tiger reserve of 3,420 ha in Assam, marking the addition of a critical habitat for the endangered tiger species, a permanent high‑priority issue under the Wildlife Protection Act.
