UPSC / Banking · Week 03 · General Awareness

08 Jun – 14 Jun, 2026

01

Highlights

Below is a concise, explanatory briefing of the most exam‑relevant happenings reported in the PIB releases for the week. Each bullet begins with a bold headline fact, followed by a brief elaboration of the what, who, when and why – the context that is frequently tested in UPSC/Banking prelims.

02

Polity & Governance

  • President Droupadi Murmu presided over the Defence Investiture 2026 (Phase‑I) at Rashtrapati Bhavan on 08 Jun 2026. Gallantry awards – 71 in total (Kirti Chakra 7, Vir Chakra 15, Shaurya Chakra 29) – were conferred on defence personnel for acts of valor between 2019‑2025.
  • Lok Sabha Speaker Om Birla inaugurated the Commonwealth Parliamentary Association (CPA) India Region‑II conference in Chandigarh on 08 Jun 2026. The theme “Viksit Bharat‑2047” underscored the role of an aware citizenry and youth‑centric skill development in nation‑building.
  • Union Finance Minister Nirmala Sitharaman met West Bengal CM Suvendu Adhikari on 08 Jun 2026. Discussions centred on credit availability and acceleration of flagship schemes – PM MUDRA Yojana, PM Jan Dhan Yojana, PM Jeevan Jyoti Bima Yojana, PM Suraksha Bima Yojana, Atal Pension Yasa and PM SVANidhi.
  • The Ministry of AYUSH celebrated International Day of Yoga 2026 (21 Jun) with the theme “Yoga for Healthy Ageing.” Joint Secretary Ms Alarmelmangai D led the launch of ten disease‑specific yoga protocols and the year‑round “Yoga 365” programme.
  • The Government’s e‑Marketplace (GeM) celebrated its 12‑year anniversary on 09 Jun 2026. MSE participation rose from 2,396 in 2016‑17 to over 11.9 lakh, with procurement from women‑owned MSEs exceeding ₹93,327 crore – a showcase of digital‑first public procurement.
  • The National e‑Governance Awards 2026 (Jaipur, 1‑2 Jul) recognised Kadepur Gram Panchayat (Maharashtra) with a Gold award for AI‑enabled, blockchain‑backed service delivery. The GP now runs 1,355 digital services for 4,321 citizens, illustrating the “Digital India” thrust.
  • The Central Government approved the General Pool Residential Accommodation (GPRA) for Amaravati on 10 Jun 2026. The 11‑tower, 1,504‑unit complex will cost ₹1,234.91 crore and meet a minimum GRIHA 4‑Star green rating – a flagship housing‑for‑officials project.
  • The Ministry of Coal roadshow in Hyderabad on 11 Jun 2026 highlighted a ₹46,000 crore scheme for surface coal and lignite gasification. The programme aims to create syngas‑based products, supporting India’s energy‑security agenda.
  • The Ministry of Panchayati Raj launched the Atmanirbhar Panchayat Programme on 09 Jun 2026. It incentivises Gram and Block‑Panchayats to prepare bank‑able, revenue‑generating projects through PPP facilitation and CSR linkage.
  • The National Human Rights Commission (NHRC) issued a su‑motu notice on 11 Jun 2026 after four workers died in a septic‑tank cleaning accident in Surat. The commission demanded a detailed report and compensation within two weeks, underscoring occupational‑safety enforcement.
  • The Cabinet Committee on Economic Affairs (chaired by the PM) approved the ₹1.25 lakh crore “Viksit Bharat GRAMP” rural‑development push on 09 Jun 2026. The allocation includes ₹30,000 crore for MGNREGA and targets 2.80 lakh Gram Panchayats for direct fund infusion.
  • The Ministry of Home Affairs, led by Amit Shah, formally launched the “Yamuna Rejuvenation Review” on 08 Jun 2026. With a 97 % desilted target of 28.57 lakh MT, the project also introduced a dairy‑waste‑to‑biogas model with NDDB.
03

Economy & Finance

  • The Economic Survey for FY 2025‑26 highlighted a real‑GDP growth of 7.7 % (FY 2025‑26) and a fiscal deficit of 5.9 % of GDP. These strong figures underpin India’s claim as the fastest‑growing major economy.
  • The Export‑Import (EXIM) Bank reported that pharmaceutical exports rose to US$ 31 billion in FY 2025‑26, aiming for US$ 50 billion by 2030. India now supplies 80‑90 % of the U.S. generic‑medicine volume, cementing its position in global health.
  • The Ministry of Finance announced that the Export Credit Guarantee Scheme (ECLGS) had issued 1,06,549 guarantees worth ₹48,484.26 crore as of 09 Jun 2026. MSMEs accounted for 96 % of the number of guarantees, reflecting the government’s credit‑enhancement policy.
  • The Credit Guarantee Scheme for Micro‑Finance Institutions‑2.0 (CGSMFI‑2.0) was extended on 10 Jun 2026 to a ceiling of ₹20,000 crore in guarantees or until 31 Aug 2026. Loan caps for large MFIs rose from ₹300 crore to ₹1,000 crore, widening the funding envelope for micro‑entrepreneurs.
  • The Consumer Price Index (CPI) for May 2026 (base 2024 = 100) showed overall inflation of 3.93 % YoY, with food inflation at 4.78 %. The highest‑inflating items were silver jewellery (+155.23 %) and tomato (+48.43 %).
  • The Ministry of Jal Shakti signed Reform‑Linked MoUs with Manipur and Bihar on 10 Jun 2026 under Jal Jeevan Mission 2.0. The agreements focus on Gram‑Panchayat‑led water governance and aim for 100 % rural tap‑water coverage by 2028.
  • The Ministry of Finance reported that the Public‑Sector Banks accounted for 96 % of the total value of guarantees under ECLGS. This concentration highlights the pivotal role of PSU banks in financing MSME growth.
  • The Ministry of New & Renewable Energy released a floating‑solar potential assessment on 10 Jun 2026, identifying > 102 GWp of floating‑solar capacity nationwide. A dedicated scheme will now channel projects through an online portal.
  • The RBI’s Financial Stability Report (FSR) for Q1 2026 noted that bank NPA ratios fell to 4.3 %, the lowest in a decade, due to stronger asset quality and improved loan‑recovery mechanisms. (The figure is from the FSR, not a press‑release, but is referenced in the Economic Survey.)
  • The Ministry of Commerce announced a target of US$ 30 billion in seafood exports by 2031, up from the current US$ 8.46 billion (≈ ₹73,890 crore). Policy levers include PLI framework, cold‑chain expansion and traceability mandates.
  • The Ministry of Skill Development & Entrepreneurship reported that 13,888 ITIs are operational, delivering 56.08 lakh+ apprenticeships since 2016. This underpins the “Skill India” pillar of Viksit Bharat.
  • The Ministry of Agriculture & Farmers’ Welfare released the 2025‑26 horticulture advance estimates on 06 Jun 2026, projecting fruit production of 1,214.75 lakh t (↑ 3.25 %). Vegetable output is also set to rise, supporting food‑security goals.
  • The Government’s “BHAVYA” industrial‑parks portal, launched on 08 Jun 2026, opened applications for 100 plug‑and‑play parks (25 acres–1,000 acres) over six years with a total outlay of ₹33,660 crore. The portal streamlines DPR submission, appraisal and monitoring for investors.
04

International Relations

  • President Narendra Modi received congratulatory messages from over 30 world leaders on 09 Jun 2026, marking his record‑long tenure of 4,398 days as Prime Minister. Leaders from Africa, Europe, Asia and the Americas highlighted India’s global partnership and development model.
  • India hosted the 13th BRICS Urbanisation Forum in New Delhi on 12‑13 Jun 2026, chaired by Union Housing & Urban Affairs Minister Manohar Lal. The forum adopted a declaration on inclusive, resilient urban futures and launched a BRICS Urban Research Network.
  • The 12th BRICS Agriculture Ministers’ Meeting took place in Indore on 12‑13 Jun 2026 under India’s 2026 BRICS chairship. Four priority themes—food security, climate‑resilient agriculture, regenerative farming, and agri‑tech—were agreed, with India pledging subsidised urea (₹266 per bag) and DAP (₹1,350 per bag).
  • India and Tajikistan signed a trade‑promotion agreement on 10 Jun 2026, reporting FY 2025‑26 exports of US$ 58.12 million (↑ 27.23 %) and services trade of US$ 123.89 million. Key exports included drug formulations, pulses and medical devices.
  • India’s foreign‑policy outreach included a high‑level visit by the Amir of Kuwait on 09 Jun 2026, focusing on West‑Asia security and the safety of the Indian diaspora. The call reinforced India’s “neighbourhood first” diplomatic stance.
  • The Ministry of External Affairs announced the launch of a cross‑border UPI‑NPI corridor with Nepal on 06 Jun 2026, enabling instant P2P transfers in nine countries. The initiative expands digital financial inclusion in the region.
  • India signed a tripartite MoU for mineral‑oil operations in the Assam–Nagaland border area on 11 Jun 2026, targeting a ten‑fold increase in production to >10,000 bbl day⁻¹. The agreement exemplifies cooperative federalism in the Northeast.
  • India’s participation in the 114th International Labour Conference in Geneva on 09 Jun 2026 was led by Labour Minister Shobha Karandlaje. Bilateral meetings with Nepal, Angola, Mauritius, France, UK, South Korea, USA and Canada focused on skills‑recognition and migration pathways.
  • The Ministry of Commerce announced that India‑EFTA Trade and Economic Partnership Agreement (TEPA) entered into force on Oct 2025, with a fresh US$ 100 billion investment commitment from Switzerland, Norway, Liechtenstein and Iceland (15 years). This reflects deepening relations with European markets.
  • India’s Navy recovered an unexploded missile warhead from the Marshall‑Islands‑flagged tanker MT Olympic Life off Oman on 07 Jun 2026, showcasing maritime security cooperation with the Omani Navy.
05

Environment & Ecology

  • The Consumer Price Index for May 2026 recorded the highest‑inflating item as silver jewellery (+155.23 %). Such spikes signal luxury‑goods price volatility that can affect consumer‑price trends.
  • The National Green Tribunal (NGT) reported that 96 % of India’s 10 kV‑class power‑transmission lines are now equipped with smart‑grid sensors as of 2026, enhancing grid reliability and reducing line losses.
  • The Ministry of Environment, Forest and Climate Change (MoEFCC) released the CAQM‑mandated air‑quality action plan on 12 Jun 2026, imposing a PM‑2.5 limit of 50 µg m⁻³ for industrial zones and accelerating the phase‑out of BS‑III diesel vehicles.
  • The Jal Jeevan Mission (JJM) reports that rural tap‑water coverage rose from 3.23 crore households (2019) to 15.86 crore households (June 2026), achieving 81.94 % coverage. The programme targets universal coverage by 2028.
  • The Ministry of New & Renewable Energy announced a floating‑solar potential of >102 GWp, representing roughly 30 % of India’s total solar potential (≈ 3,445 GWp). A dedicated floating‑solar scheme will be launched via an online portal.
  • The National Air Quality Monitoring data (CAQM) for May 2026 showed the lowest‑inflating CPI items were potatoes (‑23.71 %) and peas (‑11.47 %). Deflation in staple foods can temper overall inflationary pressure.
  • The Ministry of Water Resources executed a “Jal Sanchay Jan Bhagidari 2.0” programme, installing 1.55 crore water‑conservation and groundwater‑recharge structures nationwide by June 2026.
  • The Ministry of Environment’s Biodiversity Conservation plan listed the Ken‑Betwa River‑link project as a priority for inter‑basin water‑sharing, with a documentary archiving initiative announced on 09 Jun 2026.
  • The MoEFCC’s “One Station One Product” (OSOP) initiative started on 11 Jun 2026, setting up 28 retail stalls at high‑footfall railway stations for Divyangjan artisans, promoting inclusive livelihood.
06

Science & Technology

  • Scientists at the Institute of Nano Science & Technology (INST, Mohali) discovered that surface acoustic waves can generate spin‑currents in a 2‑D antiferromagnet, published in *Physical Review B* (DOI 10.1103/4gpb‑t3qh). The finding advances spin‑tronic device research.
  • The 3rd BRICS Neuroscience Symposium (05‑07 Jun 2026) was hosted by IIT Madras, unveiling the “Human Brainstem Atlas” (SGBC). The atlas provides cell‑resolution mapping of >200 brainstem nuclei, reinforcing India’s neuro‑research leadership.
  • The Ministry of Science & Technology (MoS & IT) released the “Rating Manual 2026” amendment on 09 Jun 2026, introducing multi‑stage assessment for under‑construction digital‑connectivity properties and aligning with the National Building Construction Standards (NBCS) 2026.
  • India’s Coal Exchange Rules 2026, published on 04 Jun 2026, created a transparent many‑to‑many trading platform for coal, shifting from a “one‑to‑many” system to improve price discovery.
  • The Defence Ministry signed a ₹449 crore contract on 10 Jun 2026 with Accord Software and Systems for 20 Enhanced Capability Global Navigation Satellite System (ECGNSS) jammers, achieving ≥ 75 % indigenous content.
  • A joint MoU between CSIR‑CRRI and the Municipal Corporation of Delhi on 10 Jun 2026 introduced the “ECOFIX” instant pothole‑repair technology using steel‑slag aggregates, promoting circular‑economy road maintenance.
  • The RISE Conclave 2026 (Bengaluru, 13 Jun 2026) showcased over 2.3 lakh start‑ups, positioning India as the world’s 3rd‑largest start‑up ecosystem, with >50 % of ventures now based in Tier‑2/3 cities.
  • The Ministry of New & Renewable Energy announced on 10 Jun 2026 a dedicated floating‑solar scheme and an online portal for small‑hydro projects, targeting ≈1,500 MW of new capacity by 2030.
  • The Indian Space Mission “Gaganyaan 2” (planned 2027) received an additional ₹1,000 crore venture‑capital fund allocation on 09 Jun 2026, reinforcing the Indian Space Policy 2023 and the IN‑SPACe framework.
07

Government Schemes & Social Sector

  • PM Jan Dhan Yojana (JDJ) now has 58.16 crore beneficiaries (May 2026), with a total balance of ₹3.02 lakh crore. The figure underscores the depth of financial inclusion.
  • Ayushman Bharat – PM JAY covered ≈ 1.43 crore families in West Bengal (08 Jun 2026), with a FY 2026‑27 resource envelope of ₹527.58 crore out of a total ₹3,505.59 crore.**
  • The Pradhan Mantri Surakshit Matritva Abhiyan (PMSMA) celebrated its 10‑year anniversary on 09 Jun 2026, having provided free antenatal services to > 7.50 crore pregnant women. Maternal mortality fell from 130/1 lakh to 87/1 lakh live births.
  • The “Garib Kalyan” 12‑year narrative (08 Jun 2026) highlighted flagship schemes such as Food‑grain Ann Yojana, PM Awas Yojana, PM Jan Dhan, MUDRA and PM SVANidhi, illustrating the integrated approach to poverty alleviation.
  • The Ministry of Tribal Affairs, together with TRIFED, launched the “RISA – Timeless Tribal” brand on 10 Jun 2026, featuring 96 companies and a ₹12,822.67 crore investment under the Textile PLI scheme.
  • The “One Station One Product” (OSOP) initiative (11 Jun 2026) under PM Vishwakarma created 28 retail stalls for Divyangjan artisans at railway stations, fostering inclusive entrepreneurship.
  • The National e‑Governance Awards 2026 (Jaipur) honored Kadepur Gram Panchayat with a ₹10 lakh Gold award for AI‑driven service delivery, showcasing the success of the Panchayat Advancement Index (PAI).
  • The Ministry of Skill Development & Entrepreneurship announced 30 Skill India International Centres on 10 Jun 2026, expanding vocational training and global placement networks.
  • The Ministry of Health & Family Welfare released a ₹75 commemorative coin and ₹5 postage stamp on 09 Jun 2026 to mark the 10‑year milestone of PMSMA.
08

Geography & Places in News

  • Indore, Madhya Pradesh, hosted the BRICS Agriculture Ministers’ Meeting (12‑13 Jun 2026) at the Indore Smart City Project venue. Delegates toured the 56‑Dukan food hub, underscoring the city’s logistics capability.
  • Sitapur, Uttar Pradesh, became the site of India’s first large‑scale 250 MW solar‑plus‑battery project on 09 Jun 2026, built on ≈ 850 acres of vacant defence land. The project aims to power defence establishments and reduce grid dependency.
  • The Zojila Tunnel (≈ 14 km, 2,900‑3,310 m altitude) in Kargil district, Jammu and Kashmir, reached a major milestone on 09 Jun 2026, cutting Sonamarg‑Minamarg travel time from ~2 hrs to ~30 min.
  • The newly commissioned 20 kW Akashvani FM transmitter in Ramgarh, Jaisalmer (11 Jun 2026) expands the FM broadcast radius to 80 km, covering ≈ 20,000 sq km of the desert region.
  • The DRDO‑advanced missile‑defence demonstration at the Indian Missile Defence Complex (12 Jun 2026) validated multi‑layered ballistic‑missile interception capabilities.
  • The Gwalior–Bhopal railway line (phase‑2) received a ₹220.51 crore investment on 09 Jun 2026, promising nine additional passenger trips per day and 2.88 MTPA freight capacity.

History, Art & Culture

  • The 19th Mumbai International Film Festival (MIFF 2026) ran from 15‑21 Jun 2026, receiving 1,459 film entries from 47 countries and awarding ₹45 lakh across 18 categories.

Awards, Sports & Miscellaneous

  • Vijay Kumar Sood, Principal Scientific Adviser, chaired the 3rd BRICS Neuroscience Symposium at IIT Madras (05‑07 Jun 2026), underscoring India’s leadership in brain‑research collaborations.
