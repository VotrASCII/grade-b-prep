Polity & Governance

The ceremony drew the Vice‑President, Prime Minister Narendra Modi, and Defence Minister, underscoring the continuity of the Ministry’s gallantry programme.
  • Presidential & Prime‑Ministerial Engagements – The PM’s tweet on 8 Jun 2026 praised the Defence Investiture ceremony, recalling the nation’s “extraordinary courage” and service, while the Vice‑President’s address at the same event highlighted the strategic importance of protection of the Indian sub‑continent.
  • Constitutional & Statutory Bodies – The High‑Court’s focus on the Nutritional Standards was reinforced by the National Human Rights Commission (NHRC) in June a‑do­t its third‑quarter report, which called for the criminalisation of digital‑arrest scams under the IT Act.
  • Parliamentary Activity – The Lok Sabha Speaker Om Birla famously launched the Commonwealth Parliamentary Association (CPA) India Region – Zone‑II conference at the Haryana Legislative Assembly on 8 Jun 2026, gathering 12 state presiding officers and discussing the “Viksit Bharat‑2047” vision.
  • Governance Initiatives – The Government released a suite of financial‑inclusion measures, launching the Direct Benefit Transfer (DBT) digital reconversion and enhancing the Jan Dhan platform, while the Financial Conduct Authority (FCA) issued a guideline for stressed‑asset management, emphasizing timely transaction circuit‑breakers.
  • Prime‑Ministerial Milestones – On 9 Jun 2026 the PM post‑humored that he was India’s longest continuously serving elected Prime Minister; his statement that 25 crore people had been lifted out of absolute poverty received praise from Malaysia, Sri Lanka, Qatar, and the EU.
  • Regional Political Engagements – The PM’s flagship speeches at Yuva Sangam Phase‑VI and the Karnataka state Ministry of Higher Education conference highlighted the “Muvud” cultural dialogue, emphasising youth entrepreneurship across three continents.
  • International Summits – The G20 presidency, held beneath Modi’s chair, saw India sign 12 new Trade‑Pact agreements, strengthening ties with Australia, New Zealand, and the UAE.
  • Election-Related Diplomatic Notes – Modi congratulated Armenia’s President Nikol Pashinyan on his parliamentary win on 8 Jun 2026, reinforcing Indo‑Armenian ties in the South Caucasus.
02

Economy & Finance

  • Economic Survey 2025‑26 Highlights – The flagship survey reported a 7.7 % real GDP growth for FY 2025‑26, noting a 3.4 % GDP‑specific pledge of ₹ 36 trn for infrastructure, and a fiscal deficit of 2.5 % of GDP, marking an improvement from the previous year’s 4.2 %.
  • Union Budget 2026‑27 – Key Allocations – The budget released in March 2026 allocated ₹ 1,89,00 crore for PM MUDRA Yojana, ₹ 81,50 crore for Jan Dhan digital banking, and ₹ 45,000 crore for a flagship Biopharma Shakti programme aimed at doubling the sector’s output by 2031.
  • RBI Policy Tweaks – The Reserve Bank of India (RBI) held the repo rate at 4.5 %, the Cash Reserve Ratio (CRR) at 4.25 %, and the Statutory Liquidity Ratio (SLR) at 22 %, sustaining inflation at a target corridor of 4 % ± 1.5 %.
  • Fertiliser Supply & Prices – On 10 Jun 2026, the Ministry of Fertilizers reported that Punjab’s Kharif 2026 requirement of 14.5 MT of urea had been fully supplied, with ₹ 266 per bag price support continuing under central subsidy.
  • Pharmaceutical Market Outlook – The Indian pharmaceutical industry was valued at US$ 60 billion in 2026, with a 7.7 % real‑GDP share, and a strategic plan to double the sector’s size over the next five years, leveraging U.S. 50 % tariffs on imports.
  • Steel Industry Performance – SAIL’s FY 2026 figures showed a +11.75 % EBITDA rise and a ₹ 8,148 crore debt reduction, while the VSP accident earlier in the week highlighted the need for stricter safety protocols and cost‑controlling mechanisms.
  • Financial Inclusion Expansion – The Jan Dhan scheme enrolled 58.16 crore beneficiaries by May 2026, with a total balance of ₹ 3.02 lakh crore transferred via DBT, showing a 60 % increase compared to FY 2025.
  • Public Procurement Digitalisation – The Government e‑Marketplace (GeM) saw a 30 % rise in orders from MSEs, with women‑owned MSEs generating ₹ 93,327 crore in procurement, boosting digital transparency.
  • Tax & Compliance – The Central GST Council approved a ₹ 48,480 crore ceiling for the Credit Guarantee Scheme for MSMEs, ensuring a 90 % coverage for new loan disbursements.
  • Infrastructure Investment – The smart‑city programme BHAVYA earmarked ₹ 33,660 crore to develop 100 industrial parks by 2032, with a projected added industrial activity of 12.5 % of the national GNVPI.

International Relations

  • G20 Presidency Highlights – Modi’s tenure as G20 Chair ended in 2026 with a record EUR 130 billion of new trade deals, focusing on digital trade and climate‑linked agriculture.
  • Quad and South Asian Tie‑Ins – The U.S.–Japan–Australia–India gathering in Bali on 10 Jun 2026 concluded joint statements on maritime security and energy transition, reinforcing Indo‑Pacific balance.
  • Europe‑India Engagements – The PM greeted the EU’s President Ursula von der Leyen on 10 Jun 2026, while the Sukarshi hub hosting 15 EU trade delegations assessed German and French technology transfer.
  • African Diplomacy – On 9 Jun 2026, Nigeria’s President Bola Ahmed Tinubu and Kenya’s President William Ruto congratulated Modi on his 123‑day service, enhancing bilateral trade in textile and commodity exports.

Environment & Ecology

  • Jal Jeevan Mission (JJM) 2.0 – On 9 Jun 2026, the Union Minister of Jal Shakti signed MoUs with Sikkim to bring untapped community‑based water governance, with a roll‑out plan of 2,000 Gram Panchayat‑led service centres.
  • Water Conservation NGO – The ISRO‑INAS launched a 30 km atmospheric‑water‑harvest pilot in Ladakh, designed to collect 3.5 m³ of potable water per year per village, supporting the Rubi‑Ward project.
  • Environmental Certification – The Indian Bureau of Standards unveiled ABS‑CHD 19 (34122) Indian Standard for school shoes, emphasizing safety and sustainability across 120 k schools nationwide.
  • Collective Climate Commitments – At the UNFCCC COP‑15, India and the BRICS quartet presented a joint 10‑year ambition to halve the use of fossil‑derived fuels, integrating Net‑Zero 2070 into the 2030 IPCC roadmap.
  • Protected Areas – The Ministry of Environment mentioned the expansion of the Kasamakaligotta National Park from 5,497 ha to 6,823 ha, adding 1,326 ha of adjacent buffer zone to safeguard tiger corridors.
  • Pollution Monitoring – The National Clean Air Programme released a 60‑day poll monitoring data showing a 3.5 % reduction in PM₂.₅ in NCR, attributed to aggressive vehicular emission controls.

Science & Technology

  • Small Hydro Power Development Scheme (SHP) – The Ministry of New & Renewable Energy announced guidelines for a 2,584.60 crore financial outlay to install an additional 1,500 MW capacity by FY 2030, with a Project Management portal for priority projects.
  • ISRO Mission Highlights – The 2026 Chandrayaan‑3 mission’s flight report on 20 Jun confirmed a successful soft‑landing on the lunar south pole, marking the first Indian personnel‑led lunar exploration after a 2‑year hiatus.
  • Digital Grid Enhancement – The Ministry of Electronics & Information Technology reported a 30 % increase in the telecom operator 5G base‑station deployments, covering 85 % of India’s population, with an allowed uplink‑downlink balance of 80 % to curb spectrum congestion.
  • Ministry of Science & Technology Initiatives – Dr Jitendra Singh launched a Centre‑of‑Excellence on Quantum Technology at CSIR‑BARC, registering 10,000 kWh‑hours/second of simulation throughput, foreshadowing strategic advances in AI acceleration.
  • Biopharma SHAKTI Programme – The Ministry announced a ₹ 10 billion federal programme to industrialise 30 Biologics by 2031, with a target of a 40 % rationalisation of active‑ingredient sourcing.
  • National Skill Development – The Ministry of Skill Development announced five new Moodle‑based training hubs for rural artisans, each with a capacity to certify 300 artisans in traditional hand‑loom weaving by 2027.

  • Ayushman Bharat – On 8 Jun 2026, West Bengal became the 36th state to fully operationalise the PM–JAY scheme, covering 1.43 crore families, with an annual contribution estimated at ₹ 1,000 crore.
  • Sustainable Agriculture Support – The Ministry for Agriculture announced a PM Kisan Samman Nidhi extension to 15 % additional stipend for 2.5 million new beneficiaries on 10 Jun 2026.

  • National Digital Health – The Digital Health Mission rolled out an interoperable data platform, aggregating 45 million health records, and decree from the Government mandated the upload of the first 5 million patient‑charts by 31 Mar 2027.
  • Women’s Welfare – The Atal Pension scheme registered 3.3 million new beneficiaries on 12 Jun 2026, including 1.2 million widows and orphans, with a 2-year subsidy of ₹ 3,000 per beneficiary.
  • Skill India – The Ministry announced a PM SVANidhi OTP‑enabled UPI settlement for ₹ 3,000 micro‑entrepreneurs per quarter, starting from 11 Jun 2026, easing last‑mile digital transactions.
