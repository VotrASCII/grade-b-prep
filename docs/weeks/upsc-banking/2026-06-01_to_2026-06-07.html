01 Jun – 07 Jun, 2026

9 topics summarised · 80 practice questions

01

Polity & Governance

  • Deputy President Shipokosa Mashatile met President D R Murmu at Rashtrapati Bhavan on 02 Jun 2026.

The meeting reiterated India‑South Africa cooperation in the UN, G20, BRICS and other multilateral forums and paved the way for a renewed India‑Africa Forum Summit.

  • The First Strategic Partnership between India and South Africa (1997) underpins the bilateral trade of ≈ USD 15.5 billion.

The partnership is being expanded to include naval exercises (IBSAMAR) and a prospective India–Southern African Customs Union (SACU) Preferential Trade Agreement. ★

  • President Murmu appointed Justice Meenakshi Madan Rai as Chief Justice of the Patna High Court (effective 05 Jun 2026).

The appointment follows the retirement of Justice Sangam Kumar Sahoo and reflects the normal rotation of high‑court chief justices.

  • Union Minister Shivraj Singh Chouhan announced that the Vikas Bharat‑Rozgar Evam Aajeevika Mission will replace MNREGA nationwide from 01 Jul 2026.

The new mission aims to broaden livelihood options beyond manual wage labour and signals a major reform in rural employment policy. ★

  • The National Panchayat Awards 2025 (conferred 03 Jun 2026) honoured 42 panchayats, of which 22 were women‑led.

The awards are administered by the Ministry of Panchayati Raj and Finance Commission 16th recommendations have boosted funding to ₹4.35 lakh cr**.

  • The Central Advisory Committee (PM‑AJAY) met on 03 Jun 2026, reporting 15 387 villages declared “Adarsh Gram”.

The committee, chaired by the Minister of Social Justice Vir Kumar, reviewed grants‑in‑aid and hostel components for SC‑beneficiary upliftment. ★

  • The National Council for Transgender Persons (NCTP) meeting (03 Jun 2026) reported 23 Garima Greh homes and > 49 000 transgender beneficiaries of skill‑development programmes.

The council highlighted progress in employment placement (821 jobs) and ongoing entrepreneurship drives. ★

  • The Supreme Court of India’s 2026 budget session saw the appointment of Justice Meenakshi Madan Rai; no major judgments were announced during the week.
02

Economy & Finance

  • India’s real GDP growth for FY 2025‑26 was recorded at 7.7 % (NSO provisional estimate, 05 Jun 2026).

The surge was driven by strong private consumption, manufacturing and services, making India the fastest‑growing large economy. ★

  • The RBI Monetary Policy Committee kept the repo rate unchanged at 5.25 % on 06 Jun 2026.

The decision reflected balanced inflation (≈ 3.5 %) and external risks from West‑Asia conflicts and a sub‑normal monsoon. ★

  • Reserve Bank of India announced a ₹10 000 cr interest‑free ATF price‑stabilisation fund for scheduled airlines (01 Jun 2026).

The scheme caps ATF price rises at 25 % and provides a cushion against volatile global fuel markets. ★

  • The Wholesale Price Index (WPI) base year was shifted from 2011‑12 to 2022‑23 (approved 25 May 2026).

Item coverage rose from 697 to 957, with solar & wind electricity now included; the revised series was released on 15 Jun 2026. ★

  • The RBI’s Variable‑Rate Repo (VRR) auction on 03 Jun 2026 allotted the full ₹11 360 cr at a cut‑off rate of 5.26 %.**

This reflects ample liquidity and the central bank’s use of flexible repo operations.

  • Foreign Portfolio Investors (FPIs) now enjoy zero tax on interest and capital gains from Government Securities effective 1 Apr 2026.

The reform expands the Fully Accessible Route (FAR) to include 15‑, 30‑ and 40‑year bonds and sovereign green bonds. ★

  • The RBI’s forward‑looking consumer‑confidence surveys (May 2026) showed inflation expectations at 3.5 % and modest optimism on employment.

These surveys feed into the RBI’s inflation‑targeting framework.

  • The Government’s ATF price‑stabilisation fund will reimburse airlines if international ATF prices exceed ₹142 / L (May 2026), a 2.5‑fold rise from ₹60.5 / L (Mar 2026).

The measure protects airline profitability and curbs pass‑through to ticket prices.

  • The PM‑Kisan scheme disbursed ₹4.28 lakh cr to 9.44 cr farmer families in FY 2025‑26, with an annual credit of ₹6 000 per family. ★
  • The Pradhan Mantri Suraksha Bima Yojana (PMSBY) recorded 57.11 cr** enrolments as of 2026, reflecting growing micro‑insurance penetration.
03

International Relations

  • India‑South Africa dialogue (02 Jun 2026) revived the India‑Africa Forum Summit and discussed a SACU Preferential Trade Agreement.

The talks also confirmed joint naval exercises under the IBSAMAR framework. ★

  • Deputy President Radhakrishnan met Nepalese leader Rabi Lamichhane (02 Jun 2026) to reinforce the “Neighbourhood First” policy.

The meeting highlighted Nepal’s election victory and bilateral cooperation in security and trade.

  • India‑Oman CEPA entered into force on 01 Jun 2026, granting duty‑free treatment to 945 tariff lines for textiles and handicrafts.

Oman imports ≈ USD 598 m of Indian textiles, making India its third‑largest supplier. ★

  • India‑UAE and India‑UK leaders exchanged views on the “India‑UK Vision 2035” and deepening strategic ties (04 Jun 2026).

The UK Foreign Secretary Yvette Cooper highlighted cooperation in green technologies and digital trade.

  • India held its first World Yogasana Championship in Ahmedabad (04 Jun 2026) with participation from 78 countries.

The event underscores India’s soft‑power outreach in sports and cultural diplomacy. ★

  • India’s first BRICS Culture Working Group meeting took place in Varanasi (04 Jun 2026) with delegates from six countries.

The group focused on creative‑economy cooperation and ethical AI for cultural content. ★

  • India‑Zimbabwe Joint Defence Committee inaugurated (04 Jun 2026) to expand training, joint manufacturing and peace‑keeping cooperation.

The MoU follows the 2025 Aero India defence‑co‑operation agreement.

  • Ramsar sites increased to 99** (up from 26 in 2014), reflecting accelerated wetland protection. ★
  • Project Tiger reserves grew to 58, supporting a tiger population of 3 682 (latest count). ★
  • The “Ek Ped Maa Ke Naam” campaign planted 262.4 crore saplings by Dec 2025, tracked via the Meri LiFE portal.
  • Namami Gange Phase II has implemented 524 projects (₹ 43 030 cr) with 355 completed, delivering 6 610 MLD of STP capacity. ★
  • India’s Green Credit Programme (GCP) identified 4 391 ha of degraded forest for restoration in 12 states (Mar 2026).
  • The National Biodiversity Authority (NBA) released ₹ 145 cr in benefits‑arising‑from‑utilisation (BAU) approvals, aiding ≈ 11 000 BMCs. ★
  • The Ministry of Environment launched 18 “Namo Oxygen Parks” in Delhi (04 Jun 2026) to improve urban air quality.
  • World Environment Day (05 Jun 2026) saw the declaration of Jai Prakash Narayan Bird Sanctuary (Uttar Pradesh) as India’s 100th Ramsar site.
