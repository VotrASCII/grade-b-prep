03

Banking & Financial Sector

*LPG & PNG*

  • Online LPG bookings ≈ 99 % (industry). Delivery‑Authentication‑Code (DAC) usage 94‑95 %.
  • 5 kg FTL cylinders sold 23.58 Lakh since Apr 2026; 1.84 Lakh via 10 400+ awareness camps (from 03‑Apr‑2026).
  • Commercial LPG allocation raised to ≈ 70 % of pre‑crisis level (including 10 % reform‑linked).
  • Penalties on LPG distributors: 349 (25 May) → 366 (06 May); suspensions 74 → 75.
  • Raids on suspected hoarding: 1 570 (04 May) → 2 100 (06 May).

*PNG*

  • Connections gasified 6.12 Lakh (Mar 2026) + infrastructure for 2.67 Lakh → total 8.79 Lakh (04 May); updated to 8.98 Lakh (06 May).
  • New customers registered 6.79 Lakh (Mar 2026).
  • Surrenders via MYPNGD.in: 43 760 (by 03 May) → 49 200 (by 05 May).

*Petroleum‑Product Supply*

  • 100 % supply assured to domestic LPG, D‑PNG & CNG (transport).
  • Excise duty on petrol & diesel cut by ₹10 / L (effective 30‑Apr‑2026).
  • Export levy reductions (Gazette 30‑Apr‑2026): Diesel from ₹55.50 → ₹23 / L, ATF from ₹42 → ₹33 / L.

*Kerosene*

  • Additional 48 000 KL allocated to states/UTs (over regular allocation). 18 states/UTs issued SKO orders; Himachal & Ladakh reported no requirement.

*Refinery & Petro‑chemical Feedstock*

  • Order dated 01‑Apr‑2026 permits oil‑refinery/petro‑chemical complexes to release minimum C₃ & C₄ streams (1 120 MT / day) from LPG pool for pharma, chemicals & paint.
  • Since 09‑Apr‑2026: ≈ 12 000 MT propylene and ≈ 1 750 MT butyl acrylate sold to pharma/chemical/paint industry.

*Post Office Modernisation (Communications)*

  • ₹5 000 crore invested in IT‑infrastructure; launch of “Advanced Postal Technology”.
  • Services now include UPI‑enabled, OTP‑based delivery, 24 × 7 counters, guaranteed 24 hr & 48 hr Speed‑Post.
  • Network: 1.65 lakh post offices; ≈ 90 crore letters & 7.5 crore parcels delivered annually; ≈ 40 crore PO Savings Bank accounts; deposits ≈ Rs 23 lakh crore.
  • First PoC‑renovated office: Nehru Place, Delhi (05‑May‑2026). 120 more PO’s identified for FY 2026‑27.

*BIS Enforcement*

  • 07‑May‑2026: Gold jewellery raid – 654 g seized (≈ ₹92 L) for lacking Hallmark Unique Identification (HUID). Penalty: imprisonment ≤ 1 yr or fine ≥ ₹1 L, up to 5× value.
  • 06‑May‑2026: Spurious toys seizure – 2 870 packs balloons + 3 000 toys without ISI mark/valid BIS licence. Penalty: imprisonment ≤ 2 yr or fine ≥ ₹2 L, up to 10× value.

*Aircraft Leasing & Financing Summit* (08‑May‑2026, GIFT City)

  • India commercial fleet ≈ 1 100 aircraft by 2027, > 2 250 by 2035; 1 640 aircraft pending delivery (≈ US$ 50 bn leasing opportunity 2026‑2035).
  • PIAO Act 2025 aligns with Cape Town Convention – speeds IDERA rights for lessors.
  • Credit Line Guarantee Scheme: ₹5 000 crore outlay to ease airline liquidity.
  • ATF price capped at +25 %, landing & parking fees cut 25 % across airports.

*Other Banking‑Related Items*

  • GRAI rankings (see RBI section).
  • TRAI Draft Telecom Consumers Complaint Redressal (Fourth Amendment) Regulation, 2026 – open for comments till 5 June 2026.
  • TRAI extension of comment dates on Satellite Communication Network Authorisation – new deadline 13 May 2026 (comments) & 27 May 2026 (counter‑comments).
  • Ministry of Mines – 101 auctioned mineral blocks operationalised (since 2015); FY 2025‑26: 212 blocks auctioned (Odisha 34, Karnataka 18, Gujarat 11, etc.).

  • 2026 Indo‑French Year of Innovation – focus on AI, applied mathematics, advanced materials, human spaceflight, ocean data.
  • Existing ISRO‑CNES joint missions (Megha‑Tropiques, SARAL, TRISHNA) and NavIC ground‑station work in France.
  • France proposes expanded collaboration on human spaceflight (training, micro‑gravity experiments).

Post Office Modernisation (07‑May‑2026) – see Banking & Financial sector.

PM‑EGP (Prime Minister’s Employment Generation Programme) (07‑May‑2026)

  • FY 2021‑26 outlay ₹13 554.42 cr fully utilised.
  • 4 03 706 micro‑enterprises set up (target 4 02 000).
  • 36.33 lakh jobs created; women beneficiaries ≈ 40 % of enterprises, 45 % of margin‑money subsidy to women; SC/ST/OBC ≈ 54 %; ≈ 80 % of enterprises in villages.

Annual Health Check‑Up Initiative for workers ≥ 40 yr (ESIC) (07‑May‑2026)

  • Launched by Union Labour Minister Dr Mansukh Mandaviya.
  • Free check‑ups for all workers ≥ 40 yr; social‑security coverage 19 % → 64 % (≈ 94 crore beneficiaries).

JANANI – Digital platform for Maternal & Child Health (07‑May‑2026)

  • Registrations: 1.34 crore beneficiaries; 30 lakh pregnant women; 30 lakh MCH cards; 1 lakh biometric verifications.

  • MoSPI updates National Accounts base year to 2022‑23.
  • States/UTs to compile GSDP with new base year; ACNAS Sub‑Committee chaired by Prof Ravindra H. Dholakia.

NITI Aayog – School Education System Report (07‑May‑2026)

  • 14.71 lakh schools, 24.69 crore students.
  • Gains in electricity, sanitation, inclusive facilities; rise in computers, internet, smart classrooms.
  • Foundations: NEP 2020, NIPUN Bharat, Samagra Shiksha.

Integrated Training for Primary Healthcare Teams (08‑May‑2026) – launched by Union Health Minister Jagat Prakash Nadda; competency‑driven framework for ASHAs, ANMs, CHOs (≈ 70 % women).

Land‑Stack Initiative (08‑May‑2026) – chaired by Union Rural Development & Agriculture Minister Shivraj Singh Chouhan. Components of Digital India Land Records Modernisation Programme 3.0: computerisation, GIS, ULPIN, Aadhaar‑seeding, mobile‑number integration, pilot urban land‑mapping.

PM MITRA Park – Warangal (Telangana) (09‑May‑2026) – first fully‑functional textile park; cost ≈ ₹1 700 crore; 5‑F model (Farm‑to‑Fibre‑to‑Factory‑to‑Fashion‑to‑Foreign).

PM MITRA Park – Warangal (08‑May‑2026) – consolidates with PLI scheme; expected ₹6 000 cr investment, ≈ 24 400 jobs.

PM MITRA Park – Warangal (10‑May‑2026) – inauguration; Project cost ₹1 695.54 cr (VGF ₹200 cr + CIS ₹300 cr); 1 327 acres, 62 % land allotted.

PM SETU (Pradhan Mantri Skilling & Employability Transformation) – NCoE for Aeronautics (08‑May‑2026) – industry consultation at NSTI Kanpur; 25+ industry bodies attended (HAL, Dassault, Tata Advanced Systems, L&T Precision).

PM Jeevan Jyoti Bima Yojana (PMJJBY) (as per latest data, 04‑May‑2026) – enrolments 27.43 crore, claims paid ₹21 512.50 crore for 10.75 lakh families; premium ₹436 / yr.
