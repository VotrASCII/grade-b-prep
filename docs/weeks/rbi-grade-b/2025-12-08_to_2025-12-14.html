RBI Grade B · Week 02 · General Awareness

08 Dec – 14 Dec, 2025

7 topics summarised · 80 practice questions

01

RBI & Monetary Policy

1. GNPA ratio (Gross Non‑Performing Assets) – 2.31 % in March 2025 (down from 11.46 % in March 2018). ★

2. NNPA ratio – 0.52 % in March 2025 (down from 6.10 % in March 2018). ★

3. CRAR (Capital to Risk‑Weighted Assets Ratio) – 17.36 % for the banking system in March 2025 (up from 12.94 % in March 2018). ★

4. Domestic deposits – ₹ 231.90 lakh cr (≈ tripled from ₹ 88.35 lakh cr in 2015). ★

5. Domestic credit – ₹ 181.34 lakh cr (≈ tripled from ₹ 66.91 lakh cr in 2015). ★

6. ECL framework – Draft RBI Directions (2025) propose moving scheduled commercial banks (incl. foreign banks) to Expected Credit Loss provisioning. ★

7. UPI share of global FPS transactions – 49 % of global real‑time payment‑volume (129.3 bn TX / 266.2 bn TX). ★

8. Digital‑payment touch‑points – 5.45 cr points rolled‑out in Tier‑3‑6 centres (as on 31 Oct 2025).

9. QR‑codes issued to merchants – 56.86 cr QR‑codes to ≈ 6.5 cr merchants (FY 2024‑25).

🔟 Digital‑lending directory – “Digital Lending Apps (DLAs)” public‑domain list launched 01 Jul 2025.

02

Banking & Financial Sector

1. Labour Code reforms (effective 21 Nov 2025) – universal minimum wage, floor‑wage, 2× overtime, 3‑year claim window, free annual health check‑up, gender‑neutral recruitment. ★

2. Emergency Credit Line Guarantee Scheme (ECLGS) – ₹ 5 lakh cr; saved 14.6 lac MSME accounts from turning NPA (≈ 98.3 % of micro & small). ★

3. NMDFC concessional loans – CL‑1: up to ₹ 20 lakh at 6 % p.a.; CL‑2: up to ₹ 30 lakh (8 % m/6 % f) – FY 2024‑25 disbursed ₹ 860.43 cr. ★

4. Smart‑meter rollout (RDSS) – 4.76 cr smart meters installed (as on 15 Nov 2025); 20.33 cr meters sanctioned.

5. AT&C loss (national) – 16.16 % in FY 2025 (target 12‑15 % by 2030). ★

6. Coal‑based capacity (as 31 Oct 2025) – 218 258 MW (43.22 % of total mix).

7. Super‑critical/USC commissioned – 70 190 MW (SC) + 7 680 MW (USC).

8. Co‑firing policy (07 Nov 2025) – 5‑7 % biomass/MSW blend permitted in coal plants.

9. Renewable‑energy push – 100 % FDI under automatic route; major schemes: PM‑KUSUM, PM‑SURYA GHAR, National Green Hydrogen Mission.

🔟 National Mission on Clean Ganga – 12 146 211 antiquities digitised; £ 20 lakh FY 2024‑25 outlay.

03

Government Schemes & Budget

1. PM‑AASHA (Pradhan Mantri Annadata Aay Sanrakshan Abhiyan) – continues under 15th FC (2025‑26).

2. Mera Gaon Meri Dharohar (MGMD) – cultural mapping of 6 38 365 villages (data for 6 23 449 uploaded).

3. National Mission on Monuments & Antiquities (NMMA) – 12 46 211 antiquities and 11 406 unprotected monuments digitised; FY 2024‑25 outlay ₹ 20 lakh.

4. National Mission for Clean Ganga (NMCG) – Digital twin at IIT‑Delhi; Mahananda River STP PPP (₹ 361.86 cr).

5. Nagar Van Yojana (NVY) – 620 vans in 28 states + 3 UTs; ₹ 5 078 4.64 cr released since 2020‑21.

6. School Nursery Yojana (SNY) – ₹ 7.38 cr released for plant‑awareness in schools.

7. ‘Ek Ped Maa Ke Naam’ – > 261 cr saplings planted (≈ 45 cr urban).

8. Plastic‑pollution drive (World Environment Day 2025) – ≈ 69 000 events, 21 lac participants.

9. E‑waste campaign – 13 cities collected 4 950 kg e‑waste.

🔟 SPARC funding for Aus‑India research – ₹ 9.84 cr for 10 joint projects (AI, quantum, med‑tech, space, defence).

04

Economy & Trade

1. Multimodal Connectivity – NPG (PM GatiShakti) – 104th NPG meeting 08 Dec 2025; projects: Gummidipundi‑Gudur (89.96 km, 25.07 MTPA), Nidadavolu‑Duvvada (198.10 km), Ghaziabad‑New Sitapur (402.78 km).

2. Carbon‑emission‑performance (BUR‑4 2020) – 2 437 Mt CO₂e, 7.93 % lower than 2019; emission intensity of GDP ↓ 36 % (2005‑2020).

3. Renewable‑energy targets – 100 % non‑fossil power target met 5 years early.

4. Electric‑vehicle incentives – GST 5 % on EVs & chargers; road‑tax waivers in several states.

5. Operation Sagar Bandhu – Indian Navy delivered 1 000 t relief cargo to Sri Lanka (four warships, 8‑Dec‑2025).

05

International Affairs & Organizations

1. UNESCO 20th ICH Committee (08‑13 Dec 2025, Red Fort) – > 150 nations; India presented 15 elements on Representative List (e.g., Vedic chanting, Yoga). ★

2. 2nd WHO Global Summit on Traditional Medicine (17‑19 Dec 2025, Bharat Mandapam) – > 100 countries; side‑event “Ashwagandha: From Traditional Wisdom to Global Impact”.

3. India‑Chile CEPA – 4th round – concluded 05 Dec 2025; aims to boost bilateral trade and market access.

4. Big‑Cat Alliance (IBCA) – 18 countries joined; India to host Global Big Cat Summit 2026.

5. Australia‑India Education & Skills Council (3rd meeting 08 Dec 2025) – MoUs with 7 Australian universities; SPARC fund ₹ 9.84 cr.

06

Awards, Rankings & Appointments

1. UNESCO Intangible Cultural Heritage – India’s 15 listed elements (e.g., Vedic chanting 2008, Ramlila 2008, Garba 2023). ★

2. UNSW (Queensland) – QS World University Ranking – 20th globally (2025).

07

Sports / Environment / Science & Tech

1. Ground‑level Ozone control – NAAQS includes O₃; BS‑VI vehicles cut NOₓ by 70‑85 % (2‑wheelers).

2. Waste‑to‑Energy (WTE) guidelines – CPCB draft mandates real‑time OCEMS monitoring in MSW‑incinerators (deadline 3 months).

3. Digital twin of Ganga – AI‑driven hydrology, groundwater stress, hotspot mapping (IIT‑Delhi).

4. Glacier‑melt study – BIHA monitors Bhagirathi & Alaknanda basins (satellite, field, SPHY models).

5. SAVE (Sage Agastya Vehicle Expedition) – 2‑Dec 2025 to 10‑Dec 2025, ≈ 2 460 km, 15‑20 vehicles, ~100 participants.
