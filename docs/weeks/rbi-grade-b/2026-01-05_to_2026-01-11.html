RBI & Monetary Policy

  • ₹50,000 cr Open‑Market‑Operation (OMO) purchase (5 Jan 2026).
    • Securities bought: 7.10 % GS 2029, 6.79 % GS 2031, 8.32 % GS 2032, 7.10 % GS 2034, 6.64 % GS 2035, 7.18 % GS 2037, 7.30 % GS 2053.
  • Variable‑Rate Repo (VRR) auctions (5‑9 Jan 2026).
    • 1‑day, 2‑day and 4‑day tenors; cut‑off/WA rate 5.26 % (full allotment).
  • Treasury‑Bill auction (9 Jan 2026).
    • 91‑day ₹9,000 cr, 182‑day ₹12,000 cr, 364‑day ₹8,000 cr; yields 5.311 % / 5.537 % / 5.581 % (cut‑off).
  • Government‑Stock auction (9 Jan 2026).
    • 6.68 % GS 2040 ₹16,000 cr & 6.90 % GS 2065 ₹13,000 cr; cut‑off yields 7.1007 % & 7.4365 %.
  • State‑Government‑Securities auction (6 Jan 2026).
    • Total issue ≈ ₹30,100 cr; average yields 7.5‑7.6 % across 10 states.
  • Revised Ways‑and‑Means‑Advances (WMA) limit (9 Jan 2026).
    • Aggregate ₹61,008 cr; Delhi’s limit ₹890 cr.
  • External‑Reserves (as on 2 Jan 2026).
    • Total ₹6,194,458 cr (≈ US$687 bn); Gold ₹1,003,513 cr.
  • Financial‑Stability‑Report (FSR) – GNPA trend (Sept 2025).
    • SCBs GNPA 2.22 % (₹4.18 L cr), PSBs GNPA 2.30 % (₹2.65 L cr). ★
  • RBI circular 2025‑26 on Urban‑Co‑operative Banks (UCBs).
    • New‑branch auto‑route: up to 10 branches; PSL target lowered to 60 %; housing‑loan exposure ↑ 10 %→ 25 % (max ₹3 cr); gold‑loan ceiling ↑ ₹2 lac → ₹4 lac. ★
  • RBI circular 2025‑26 on NBFCs/HFCs.
    • Credit‑rating requirement, ombudsman thresholds, 50 % credit‑share target for NBFCs by 2047, green‑finance push. ★
  • Payments‑Regulatory‑Board (first meeting) – “Payments Vision 2028”. (5 Jan 2026).
  • Agreement with GNCT‑D (9 Jan 2026). – RBI to manage Delhi’s rupee‑public‑debt and banking operations. ★
