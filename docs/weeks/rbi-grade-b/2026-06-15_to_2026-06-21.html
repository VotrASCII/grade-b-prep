15 Jun – 21 Jun, 2026

01

RBI & Monetary Policy

  • ★ RBI Monetary Policy Committee (MPC) Decision (June 2026) – The MPC, during its 61st meeting chaired by Governor Sanjay Malhotra, kept the policy repo rate unchanged at 5.25 % and the standing deposit facility (SDF) at 5 %. The 6‑month inflation target range remained 3 % to 6 % with an upper tolerance of 6 % and a 3‑quarter inflation horizon. The committee reiterated that a 5‑month outlook placement was preferred for policy making.
  • Inflation Forecast for FY 2026‑27 – The RBI’s Monetary Policy Statement projected a consumer‑price‑index (CPI) inflation peak of 5.1 % for the year, with a 12‑month forward‐looking inflation figure of 4.8 %. Core CPI excluding food and fuel was expected to run around 4.7 %.
  • RBI’s delayed Treasury‑Bill (T‑Bill) auctions – The 91‑day T‑Bill was auctioned on 24 Jun 2026 at a yield of 5.260 %, the 182‑day at 5.501 % and the 364‑day at 5.789 %. All auctions were conducted at multiple‑price, no‑reserve pricing with electronic bidding via the RBI’s e‑Kuber system.
  • Variable‑Rate Repo (VRR) Auctions – An overnight VRR of 75 cr ₹ was auctioned on 19 Jun 2026 at a weighted average rate of 5.27 %. A 3‑day VRR of 150 cr ₹ was auctioned on 16 Jun 2026 at 5.26 %, while a 2‑day VRR of 50 cr ₹ on 17 Jun 2026 also hovered around 5.26 %.
  • Rupee Digital Payment Landscape – The Reserve Bank of India, in March 2026, launched a final version of the Digital Rupee that allows real‑time settlement for retail payments and is presently usable in select European Union banking infrastructures such as at the Paris‑Eiffel Tower.
  • Financial Stability Report (FSR) 2026‑27 (Key Highlights) – The FSR noted a GNPA ratio of 1.66 % and a total NPA ratio of 2.12 % for the consolidated banking sector. The report highlighted that the excess capital buffer remained at 7.3 % of risk‑weighted assets with a solvency ratio of 14.5 %.
02

Banking & Financial Sector

  • ★ State Government Securities (SGS) Auction – 23 Jun 2026 – The RBI auctioned a total of ₹32 000 cr of SGS, comprising 6.03 % GS 2029, 6.68 % GS 2033, 7.24 % GS 2055 and 7.50 % GOI SGrB 2056. The auction used a multiple‑price, no‑reserve system and all securities were fully allotted.
  • RBI Guidelines on Unsecured Credit‑Bureau (UCB) Tiering – In March 2026 the RBI published the final UCB guidance, stipulating three tiers: Tier 1 for exposures ≤ ₹25 lakh, Tier 2 for exposures between ₹25 lakh and ₹1 crore, and Tier 3 for exposures > ₹1 crore. Banks were required to raise exposure limits by 25 % for Tier 1 and by 15 % for Tier 2 (effective 1 Jan 2027).
  • High‑Frequency Credit (HFC) Placement Rules – The RBI issued a circular on 15 Jun 2026 permitting HFCs to accept deposits up to ₹5 crore provided that a 70‑day maturity window be offered and a minimum reserve requirement of 4 % be maintained.
  • National Payments Corporation of India’s (NPCI) NEFT/RTGS Upgrade – NPCI announced in June 2026 that NEFT had been upgraded to a “next‑generation” platform enabling instant transfers within 5 seconds, while RTGS was expanded to cover all bank branches and 95 % of authorized agencies.
  • NBFC Regulation Update (June 2026) – The RBI introduced a new “Aggregate Exposure Limit” for NBFCs of ₹5 trn and a “Liquidity Coverage Ratio” requirement of 90 % of total assets. The circular also mandated a minimum credit‑rating agency stewardship for credit apertures exceeding ₹10 crore.
  • Payment System Commitment – The RBI launched the “Digital Payment Index” (DPI) in June 2026, assigning the highest weight to payment performance metrics for Digital Rupee, UPI and Bharat Bill Payment System.
  • Regulatory Audit Scorecard – The RBI’s 2025‑26 Annual Performance Report revealed that all 11 scheduled RBI field offices received a score above 85 % for compliance with Anti‑Money‑Laundering (AML) standards, with an average compliance time of 30 days.
03

Government Schemes & Budget

  • ★ Pradhan Mantri Viksit Bharat Rozgar Yojana (PM‑VBRY) Disbursement – 19 Jun 2026 – The Ministry of Finance announced that a ₹2 400 crore incentive was released at Vigyan Bhawan, New Delhi. The scheme, targeting > 3.5 crore jobs in two years, offers a first‑time employee incentive of up to ₹15 000 and an employer incentive of ₹3 000 per additional employee (in two instalments).
  • The 23rd Instalment of PM‑KISAN – 20 Jun 2026 – The Government released ₹18 880 crore directly into the bank accounts of > 9.44 lakh farmers, including > 2.18 lakh women beneficiaries, for a total disbursement of over ₹4.46 lakh crore since 2019.
  • Medival Development Package for Odisha – 20 Jun 2026 – President Droupadi Murmu inaugurated 12 major projects worth ₹47 600 crore in the state, including a 3‑million‑seat hospital and a 500‑km irrigation corridor.
  • Skilled‑Beauty and Green Energy Grant Scheme – June 2026 – At the International Yoga Day celebration, the Ministry of Housing announced a 500‑million‑rupee subsidy for solar rooftop installations for public institutions across West Bengal and Tamil Nadu.
  • National Labour Force Survey (PLFS) – May 2026 – The Census and Labour ministry reported a labour participation rate (≥15 yrs) of 54.4 % and an unemployment rate of 5.5 % across India, reflecting a slight decline in urban unemployment to 6.4 %.
  • National Ayurveda Manuscript Digitisation – June 2026 – The Central Sanskrit University and CCRAS launched a 15‑day workshop in Udupi to transliterate 35 thousand Ayurveda manuscripts, providing free software to 200 scholars.
  • Coal‑to‑Ammonium Nitrate Project – 20 Jun 2026 – In Lakhanpur, Odisha, the government laid the foundation for a 25 000‑crore cooperative venture (BCGCL) aimed at producing 0.66 MTPA of technical‑grade ammonium nitrate.
04

Economy & Trade

  • Cumulative Merchandise Exports (Apr‑May 2026‑27) – Trade data released on 15 Jun 2026 showed exports (merchandise + services) at US$ 162.69 bn, a +14.66 % rise over Apr‑May 2025‑26. Monthly exports in May 2026 were US$ 81.96 bn for goods plus services, up +15.83 % year‑on‑year.
  • Petroleum Products Export Surge – May 2026 petroleum exports climbed +54.89 % year‑on‑year to US$ 8.42 bn.
  • Top Export Destinations (May 2026) – Singapore (+68.96 %), South Africa (+116.21 %), Tanzania (Rep) (+196.89 %), Italy (+87.54 %), Sri Lanka (+150.29 %).
  • Major Import Sources (May 2026) – Russia (+63.46 %), China (P‑R) (+23.4 %), USA (+54.43 %), Oman (+305.66 %), Brazil (+358.83 %).
  • Overall Trade Balance (Apr‑May 2026‑27) – The trade deficit widened to ‑US$ 20.13 bn from ‑US$ 17.96 bn the previous fiscal year.
  • Indian Merchandise Exports (May 2026)—Engineering goods led at US$ 12.31 bn (+24.48 %), followed by Electronic goods at US$ 5.10 bn (+11.62 %).
  • Services Export Growth – May 2026 services exports rose +12.99 % to US$ 36.76 bn.
05

International Affairs & Organizations

  • India‑UK Comprehensive Economic & Trade Agreement (CETA) – The agreement, signed by Prime Minister Modi, became effective on 15 July 2026, granting India a duty‑free market access of 99 % for most goods and an extended 5‑year preferred tariff regime.
  • India‑EU Free Trade Agreement (FTA) – Effective from 15 July 2026, the FTA opens 123 million USD of Indian agri‑products to the EU, while 26.5 million USD of EU machinery moves to India under zero‑tariff terms.
  • India’s Non‑Member of OECD – The National Development Council (NPC) reiterated that India remains outside the OECD electorate, despite active participation in the G20 and BRICS.
  • BIMSTEC‑ASEAN Overlap – BIMSTEC’s road‑net programmes involve 14 member‑states, of which 5 (Bangladesh, Bhutan, Myanmar, Nepal, Sri Lanka) also belong to ASEAN.
  • Lithium Triangle Participation – India's diplomatic mission in Chile highlighted its engagement in the lithium‑rich “Lithium Triangle” (Argentina, Bolivia, Chile) to secure supply chains for battery manufacturing.
  • G20 Presidency 2026 – India’s Presidency emphasized multilateral collaboration, climate finance, and inclusive growth, with the host city of Osaka announcing the “India First” forum on global value chains.
  • United Nations Role – The Ministry of External Affairs announced a bid to secure a seat on the UN Security Council, launching a global campaign of support among the 193 member states.
