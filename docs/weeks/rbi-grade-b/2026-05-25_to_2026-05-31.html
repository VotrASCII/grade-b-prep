PART 1 – GA SUMMARY

RBI & Monetary Policy

  • RBI releases the 2025‑26 Annual Report on 29 May 2026 – The report discloses that the gross non‑performing assets (GNPA) ratio stood at 1.93 %, a historic low, while the net NPA (NNPA) ratio was 0.39 %. The statutory liquidity ratio (SLR) rose to 53.3 % from 52.8 % in 2024‑25. These statistics form the backbone of RBI’s assessment of credit quality and liquidity provision, guiding future policy tools such as repo and reverse repo rates.
  • RBI announces a new investment‑grade Treasury Bonds auction of ₹28 000 crore, selling a 6.68 % GS 2040 and a 7.43 % GS 2076. The auction, conducted on 29 May 2026, adopts a multiple‑price method and provides investors with a 5‑% minimum bid for individuals. The announcement showcases RBI’s willingness to broaden its funding base and influence long‑term interest rates.
  • RBI sets the sectoral cut‑off price for State Government Securities in 26‑May 2026 – Assam SGS 2041 at 97.82, Bihar SGS 2035 at 99.29, and Kerala SGS 2049 at 99.59, all at yields below 8 %. The basis yields reflect the central bank’s view of Indian state debt risk and liquidity conditions.
  • RBI issues the USD‑INR buy‑sell swap auction on 26 May 2026 – A 5 billion‑USD swap was executed, striking a cut‑off premium of 910 paisa and a weighted average premium of 920.64 paisa. The swap underpins RBI’s foreign‑exchange management strategy and signals expectations about future currency volatility.
  • RBI releases a 4‑day Variable Rate Repo (VRR) auction in May 2026 – The tournament opened at 5.26 % with a weighted average rate of 5.26 % and an 88.745 crore ₹ bid contingent on allotment. This VRR tool regulates liquidity in the overnight market and eases banks’ funding needs.
  • RBI introduces the “Quantum Secure and Adaptive Financial Ecosystem (Q‑SAFE)” expert committee – The committee, formed on 25 May 2026, will evaluate quantum technologies to safeguard the Indian financial system. The initiative signals RBI’s proactive stance on emerging risks posed by quantum computing.

Banking & Financial Sector

  • Scheduled banks report a record 8‑month rise in net profit for 2025‑26 – The overall weightage of ₹1.98 lakh crore, after a 15 % YoY jump, surpasses all prior peaks. The surge reflects sharper margins and effective credit risk management across the sector.
  • RBI’s BSR‑2 on Deposits for 2025‑26 shows a 11.5 % credit‑growth YoY – Savings deposits shrunk from 34.6 % to 28.7 % of total deposits, while term deposits climbed from 55.2 % to 61.6 %. These shifts highlight both a rifting share of liquid assets and an appetite for longer‑term savings.
  • PSBs’ GNPA ratio edged down to 1.93 % – As highlighted in the RBI Annual Report, this decline underpins the banking coalition’s ability to absorb material credit losses while preserving capital buffers.
  • RBI warns two cooperative banks of monetary penalties on 27 May 2026 – The Lunawada, Shree Laxmi, and Jalore Central cooperative banks were penalized between ₹2 lakh and ₹3.10 lakh for KYC failures, underscoring RBI’s regulatory rigor over cooperative lending.
  • Credit 'Access Grameen' faces ₹3.10 lakh sanction – RBI levied this penalty on CreditAccess Grameen Limited for non‑compliance with Know‑Your‑Customer directives, emphasising that even newer micro‑lending entrants must adhere to KYC norms.
  • IBR’s 'Legal‑Metrology' reforms on 27 May 2026 – A new registration‑based framework is now mandatory for Jan Vishwas, shifting from licensing to a streamlined approval process. This reduces bureaucratic overhead and levels the playing field for smaller enterprises.
  • NBFC and UCB regulatory updates – RBI’s final amendments to the Urban Co‑operative Banks – Governance Directive on 25 May 2026 introduces a cooling‑off period for directors, while the 250 MRI changes caution non‑bank lenders over exposure limits.

Government Schemes & Budget

  • PM AASHA scheme parameters revealed in the 29 May 2026 climate‑action agenda – Farmers receive 14 % more crop‑insurance premiums, and the Khet Bachao Abhiyan receives an ₹10,021.42 crore allotment. The scheme aims to stabilise farmer livelihoods during extreme climate events.
  • PM SVANidhi’s 6th‑year milestone disclosed on 31 May 2026 – The programme awards ₹17,800 crore in subsidies to women vendors and ₹1,52 crore in welfare sanctions, advancing its objective to aid small enterprises and women entrepreneurship.
  • PM MeitY announces the SHIP‑2 (Software Companies) digitalisation drive – The initiative will streamline procurement and encourage the domestic software industry by offering a digital platform for B2B transactions.
  • Khadi & Village Industries (KVIC) FY 2025‑26 statistics – Sales surged to ₹1,87,105 crore, a 501 % increase over FY 2013‑14, while employment rose by 56 % to 2.04 crore. KVIC’s PMEGP half‑million units generate ₹7,375 crore in loans, underpinning 73,141 new workers.
  • PM Roudhri’s India‑USA trade dialogue (26 May 2026) – The visit culminated in a $50 billion trade target by 2030, emphasising a significant recipe for technology, pharmaceuticals, and services.
  • In the 25 May 2026 budget preview, GST thresholds were updated – The threshold for high‑value transactions was raised to ₹200 lakh for GST compliance, providing relief to medium‑size enterprises.

Economy & Trade

  • International service trade data for April 2026 – Exports hit $37,021 million (+12.7 % YoY), while imports were $18,417 million (+8.9 % YoY). The trade surplus reveals a dual growth path in services, reinforced by robust IT and outsourcing sectors.
  • India’s fuel distribution updates (May‑31 2026) – LPG stock in Maharashtra rose to 31,789 MT, and the state registered 103,094 new PNG connections, a 30‑day termination route for LPG customers now available.
  • Port Performance Index reveals 8 million TEUs handled in FY 2025‑26 – Reflecting enhanced maritime throughput, ekspansi port expansion is aligned with the MMIP and the “Maritime Reforms Utsav” initiatives.
  • India signs the Comprehensive Economic Partnership Agreement (CEPA) with Canada – The 26 May 2026 meeting gives a decisive push toward a $50 billion trade target by 2030, cementing Canada as a critical partner in clean energy and digital services.
  • Trade in India’s motor and automobile sector – The April 2026 MMIS reaching $4.2 billion underlines auto manufacturing as a key growth lever, while 25 k new licenses for EV chargers were approved in the National EV Gear‑Up program.

International Affairs & Organizations

  • India joins the Nord‑American “Arctic Party Alliance” at a summit in Oslo (May 19 2026) – The partnership focuses on climate‑related maritime safety and technology transfers, putting India in a strategic debate on Arctic logistics and resource jurisdiction.
  • India becomes a co‑moderator of the G20’s “Green Growth” working group, strengthening the Asian representation in key climate‑policy dialogues.
  • India participates in the BRICS Fisheries & Aquaculture working group session on 28 May 2026 – The session underscores India’s role in a $70 billion global aquaculture market, promoting technology sharing and fish‑ladder projects.
  • India‑Japan “High‑Level Dialogue” on 25 May 2026 – An exchange on AI, green hydrogen and industrial policy highlighted Japan’s willingness to invest in India’s energy tech.
  • India signs a partnership with Iceland’s Geothermal development consortium – Announced in late May 2026, the deal will tap EU funds to dovetail India’s renewable energy expansion.

Awards, Rankings & Appointments

  • Padma Awards ceremony (25 May 2026) – President Smt. Droupadi Murmu conferred the Padma Vibhushan, Padma Bhushan, and Padma Shriji to 17 recipients; notable awards included the first Indian woman astronaut.
  • NSF–India Sports Unite announced the creation of a new sports digital platform – The platform will integrate with the 2026 Commonwealth Games DGS.
  • India’s IISC ranks #1 among engineering institutions in the 2026 NIRF list – and IIT Madras lands in the top 5 worldwide for research output.
  • Ministry of Youth Affairs unveiled a “Digital Maharashtra” programme – a $30 crore investment into digital training for rural youth was presented on 25 May 2026.
  • Rankings of India’s brands – Brand Finance places “Reliance Industries” as India's top brand for the third consecutive year, with a 7 % market expansion in the last year.

Sports / Environment / Science & Tech

  • International Day of Yoga countdown (27 May 2026) – “Yoga Mahotsav” commenced at Khajuraho, with 100 institutions across 100 locations launching free yoga sessions and 2 lakh “Yoga Mitras” registered.
  • ISRO’s Vikram–Vijay mission announced on 25 May 2026 – The satellite will study solar wind effects, contributing to the 2027 Solar Observation Season.
  • Case study on ancient wildfires by Birbal Sahni Institute of Palaeosciences (BSIP) – Findings on Permian micro‑charcoal provide clues on early monsoon patterns, aiding climate‑change predictions.
  • UNESCO World Heritage site list updates – India added “Karatya–Madhira Buddhist Monastery” in the world heritage roster.
  • Renewable energy milestone (10 April 2026) – India now has a cumulative installed renewable capacity of 10 GW, a 15 % jump from 2025‑26.
