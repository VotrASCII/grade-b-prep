01

RBI & Monetary Policy

  • ★ Repo rate cut by 100 bps in FY 26 (Apr‑Dec 2025) to 5.25 %.
  • ★ Cash‑Reserve Ratio (CRR) reduced by 100 bps to 3.0 % (Sept‑Nov 2025).
  • Liquidity operations in Jan 2026: OMO purchase of ₹50,000 cr of government securities; net injection ₹58,740 cr.
  • Variable‑Rate Repo (VRR) auctions (90‑day) in Jan 2026 with weighted‑average rate 5.34 %.
  • Money‑stock (M3) as of Jan 2026: ₹29.20 lakh cr (‑1.0 % YoY).
  • GNPA ratio of scheduled commercial banks at a multi‑decadal low of 2.2 % (Sept 2025).
  • RBI‑ESMA MoU signed on 27 Jan 2026 to cooperate on Central Counterparties.
  • Safe‑harbour margin for IT‑services increased to 15.5 % with threshold ₹2,000 cr (Budget 2026‑27).
02

Banking & Financial Sector

  • SME Growth Fund announced: ₹10,000 cr to create “Champion SMEs”.
  • TReDS made mandatory for CPSEs to settle MSME invoices; CGTMSE guarantee extended to TReDS receivables.
  • Municipal‑Bond incentive: ₹100 cr for a single bond issue >₹1,000 cr.
  • Power Finance Corp. (PFC) and Rural Electrification Corp. (REC) slated for restructuring (Banking Committee).
  • Advanced Pricing Agreement (APA) for IT services fast‑tracked – closure within 2 years (extendable 6 months).
  • Buy‑back tax clarified: 22 % for promoters, 30 % for non‑promoters.
  • Corporate Mitras Initiative: professional bodies to train “Corporate Mitras” for Tier‑II/III towns.
  • TCS rate on scrap & minerals reduced to 2 % (Budget 2026‑27).
  • Safe‑harbour for foreign cloud‑service firms using Indian data‑centres: tax holiday until 2047.
  • Deposit‑rate (1‑yr MCLR) as of Jan 2026: 8.40 %; WALR on fresh rupee loans 8.28 %.
03

Government Schemes & Budget

  • ★ Fiscal deficit targeted at 4.3 % of GDP for FY 2026‑27 (down from 4.4 %).
  • ★ Revenue deficit projected at 0.8 % of GDP.
  • Capital expenditure (Capex) for FY 2026‑27: ₹12.2 lakh cr (↑ from ₹11.2 lakh cr).
  • PM‑SYM (Pradhan Mantri Shram Yogi Maan Dhan): voluntary pension, government match 1:1, assured ₹3,000 per month at 60 y.
  • SVAMITVA: ≈ 3 crore property cards issued in 1.84 lakh villages (land‑record digitisation).
  • PM‑VIKAS (Pradhan Mantri Virasat Ka Samvardhan) – demand‑driven skilling, AI‑based tribal‑language translator “Adi Vaani”.
  • PM‑Kisan (formerly PM‑KUSUM) – ₹1.70 lakh cr fertilizer subsidy outlay.
  • India‑EU Free Trade Agreement (FTA) signed on 27 Jan 2026; > 99 % of Indian export value gets market‑access, zero duty on $33 bn of goods.
  • National Ayush Mission (NAM) – integration of AYUSH systems with digital tools; > 12,000 AYUSH Wellness Centres operational.
  • PM GatiShakti & National Logistics Policy – multimodal logistics platform, digital “GatiShakti” dashboard.
  • Railway safety – ₹1,306 km of Kavach 4.0 ATPS commissioned (record single‑day commissioning).
  • Dedicated Freight Corridor (Dankuni‑Surat) – 2,052 km, cost ₹1.20 lakh cr.
  • High‑speed rail corridors – 7 routes covering ~4,000 km; total investment ≈ ₹16 lakh cr.
04

Economy & Trade

  • Real GDP growth FY 26 (first advance estimate): 7.4 %; FY 27 forecast 6.8‑7.2 %.
  • Exports FY 25 (merchandise + services): USD 825.3 bn; services export USD 387.5 bn (+13.6 %).
  • India’s share in global merchandise exports: ≈ 1.8 % (2024).
  • Remittances FY 25: USD 135.4 bn – world’s largest recipient.
  • Foreign‑exchange reserves (16 Jan 2026): USD 701.4 bn (≈ 11 months import cover, 94 % external debt covered).
  • Current‑account deficit (H1 FY 26): 0.8 % of GDP (down from 1.3 %).
  • FDI FY 25: USD 64.7 bn (record greenfield).
  • Sector‑wise tariff concessions in India‑EU FTA – textiles, gems‑jewellery, marine products, chemicals; immediate duty‑free on ≈ 70 % of export value.
  • Power sector – installed capacity ≈ 509.7 GW (49.8 % renewable share).
  • National Waterways – increase from 5 to > 100 waterways.
