01 Jun – 07 Jun, 2026

01

RBI & Monetary Policy

• RBI’s Monetary Policy Committee (MPC) decided to keep the policy repo rate unchanged at 5.25 % for the 61st meeting (June 3‑5 2026). The standing‑deposit facility (SDF) was held at 5.00 % and the marginal‑standing facility (MSF) and bank rate at 5.50 %.

• The Reserve Bank announced the final interest rate on the new 6.94 % Government‑Security 2036 (FRB 2036) to be 6.64 % per annum for the half‑year period June 7 – December 6 2026, with a weighted‑average yield of 6.9686 %.

• RBI held a 4‑day Variable‑Rate Repo (VRR) auction on 8 June 2026, notifying ₹75 000 crore for the 4‑day tenor; the award rate was 5.26 %.

• The RBI approved a new auction of 14 800 crore in State‑Government Securities (SGS) on 9 June 2026, with 13 states participating across tenors from 12 to 20 years.

• An auction of 32 000 crore 91‑Day, 182‑Day and 364‑Day Treasury Bills was held on 10 June 2026; the weighted‑average yields were 5.5586 %, 5.5471 % and 3.6768 % respectively.

• RBI released the 2026‑27 *Forward‑Looking Surveys* (Urban Consumer Confidence, Inflation Expectation, Rural Consumer Confidence, Macroeconomic Forecasts) on 5 June, with the latest CPI inflation forecast for 2026‑27 at 5.1 % and core inflation at 4.7 %.

• The RBI welcomed the *Sahamiṭi Foundation* as the first Self‑Regulatory Organisation (SRO) for the Account Aggregator ecosystem (SRO‑AA) on 5 June 2026.

• RBI’s “Citizen's Charter” showed a 99.9 % processing time for applications in May 2026, with a total of 18 763 applications received.

• The RBI imposed a monetary penalty of ₹41.80 lakh on Canara Bank on 5 June 2026 for non‑compliance with KYC and “Inoperative Accounts” directives.

• RBI issued *Directions under Section 35A* to extend the period for Nasik Jilla Mahila Vikas Sahakari Bank for three more months on 5 June 2026.

• A “renewable‑energy‑based” 5‑GW data‑centre project by AirTrunk (US$30 bn) was announced by the PM on 5 June 2026 in a tweet.

• RBI’s *Gold holdings* declaration on 3 June 2026 affirmed that physical gold stock remains at 880.52 t, unchanged since the last bulletin.

02

Banking & Financial Sector

• The RBI announced the deadline for the 2023‑26 *Survey on Foreign Liabilities and Assets of Mutual Funds* on 1 June 2026, with the submission deadline on 7 July 2026.

• The RBI’s *Survey on ITES Exports* (2025‑26) will be returned by 15 July 2026, covering computer software and ITES/BPO.

• The RBI re‑appointed Shri Swaminathan Janakiraman as Deputy Governor on 5 June 2026, to serve until 26 June 2026.

• The *Sovereign Gold Bond – Series‑III* allows premature redemption from 8 June 2026 at ₹15 512 per unit (simple average of the closing price of gold on 3–5 June 2026).

• RBI launched a *Retro‑active* waiver for ATF: a ₹10 000 cr interest‑free budgetary support to Indian Airlines via IOC to stabilise ATF prices (jurisdiction from 5 June 2026).

• RBI’s *Sense of Penalty* on Puran Associates Private Ltd (₹3.10 lakh) for mis‑classification of accounts on 5 June 2026 showed stringent enforcement on asset quality.

• RBI announced that the *Underwriting auction* for the 6.94 % GS 2036 on 5 June 2026 had 382 competitive bids, cutting‑off price of 99.79 (YTM 6.9686 %).

• The RBI’s *Interest Rate on Deposits Amendment Directions 2026* were opened to comments until 20 June 2026 (affecting CB, SFB, RRB, PB, LCB, UCB).

• RBI announced that *20 % of the 2025‑26 Treasury Bill issuance* would be in supportive market for DFIs – 12 000 crore (91‑day) and 12 000 crore (182‑day).

• RBI decreed that *Balam* pays interest on the 2026‑27 Government‑Security 6.94 % for the quarter at 6.96 % (price 99.79).

• The RBI’s *Account Aggregator Ecosystem* notice (5 June) approved Sahamiṭi Foundation, marking India’s first SRO‑AA under the 2025 SRO framework.

03

Government Schemes & Budget

• PM‑KISAN of 2026 disbursed ₹4.28 lakh crore through 22 installments, benefiting 9.44 cr families (over 25 % women).

• PM‑FBY continued to insure > 4 cr farmers annually, with claims worth ₹1.96 lakh crore in FY 2025‑26.

• The *2025‑26 national budget* projected real GDP growth at 7.6 % with macro‑policy emphasis on manufacturing and services; the CPI infl. was expected to rise to 5.1 % at year‑end.

• The *National Trade Data* announced on 5 June 2026 showed exports of $95.1 m in textiles‑apparel to Oman (a 3 % growth vs FY 2025‑26).

• The *Horticulture value added* increased to ₹369.05 cr in 2024‑25, representing 37 % of crop‑sector GVA.

• The 2025‑26 *Ujjwala Yojana* announced the addition of 22.42 lakh free LPG connections in the first quarter.

• The *Sunkar* plan for 2026–27 announced an outlay of ₹1.28 lakh cr for the *PM‑Madhura Ghar* scheme (solar‑rented houses).

• The *Prime Minister’s Youth Convention* (MY Bharat) on 6 June 2026 brought > 6,000 participants and set a record of 390,812 quiz participants in the week.

• The *Stand‑Up India* scheme continues to support 2.05 lakh women entrepreneurs, raising 44 % in profited beneficiaries in the past 5 yrs.

• Rising collaboration with the UK: UK Foreign Secretary Yvette Cooper met PM Modi on 4 June, reaffirming the “India‑UK Vision 2035.”

• NATO & UN attendance: India remains a *non‑member* of NATO but holds a *Permanent Representative* role at the UN Security Council.

• India’s 2026 G20 Presidency saw the launch of the *“Pink Cup”* partnership with the UK in sports.

• BRICS Culture WG on 4 – 5 June 2026 in Varanasi produced an action plan on “Sustainable cultural heritage.”

Economy & Trade

• Real GDP in FY 2025‑26: ₹323.12 lakh crore (constant prices), a 7.6 % rise over FY 2024‑25; nominal GDP ₹346.36 lakh crore, 8.9 % growth.

• WPI base year changed from 2011‑12 to 2022‑23 (approved 25 May 2026), with item count rising from 697 to 957 and methodology shifting to GVO weights and chain‑based formulation.

• Merchandise exports in April 2026: textiles & apparel reached USD 95.1 m (up 3 % vs FY 2025‑26); fisheries exports hit ₹73,890.46 cr (USD 8.45 bn).

• NIMC‑UK Critical Minerals GSCO launched on 4 June 2026, providing a data‑driven platform for critical‑mineral supply‑chain risk assessment.

• LPG & PNG supply: 4 June 2026 report showed no dry‑outs, with 19,382 MT commercial LPG sold in 3 days and 649 MT auto‑LPG sold.

• Inflation expectations: CPI core inflation forecast for 2026‑27 is 4.7 %, while headline CPI expected to rise to 5.1 %.
