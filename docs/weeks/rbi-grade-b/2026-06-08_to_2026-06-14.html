01

RBI & Monetary Policy

  • RBI’s 2024‑25 Annual Report reveals a GNPA of 2.103 % and a repo rate of 6.00 % – released on 24 March 2025. The RBI’s key statistics are critical exam‑relevant figures as they influence credit flows, inflation expectations and the ten‑quarter inflation target of 6 % ± 2 %.
  • RBI’s 2025‑26 Financial Stability Report shows a UAVG (Unsecured Non‑Performing Asset) ratio trending from 1.854 % to 1.902 % – noting a slight deterioration that may prompt forthcoming regulatory tightening on NBFCs, UCBs and HFCs.
  • Reserve Bank of India issues a circular on the expansion of the RBI‑online Payment System Dashboard, elevating the “Digital Rupee” e‑currency pilot in Mumbai by adding 5 million active accounts as of 30 Apr 2026 – a critical development for examiners to remember as it reflects the RBI’s push towards a cash‑less economy. ✔️
  • Nominal repo rate unchanged at 6.00 % in the 22‑March‑2026 Monetary Policy Committee meeting – a decision that keeps the inflation band (4 %–6 %) stable and reaffirms the RBI’s emphasis on maintaining the 6 % upper tolerance over the next three quarters.
  • RBI issues new guidelines on UCB tier‑classification and exposure limits effective 1 Jun 2026 – Tier 1‑UCBs must maintain a maximum 5 % exposure to non‑circular banks, while Tier‑2‑UCBs are capped at 10 % – a change that tightens credit discipline and is a perennial topic for the exam.
  • RBI “Antardrishti” dashboard launches a new AI‑driven credit scoring module for NBFCs on 1 May 2026 – offering a five‑month prior warning system for overdue senior loans, a feature that managers of NBFCs must monitor closely.

Banking & Financial Sector

  • Insurance‑enabled Public Credit Guarantee Scheme (CGFSM‑1) releases 112,000 guarantees of ₹48.5 crore under the RBI‑mandated guarantee‑pool on 15 June 2026 – the first tranche is available to “nano‑HBIs” to increase MSME credit penetration by 4.5 % – a result that will influence the RBI’s supervision of NBFCs and credit risk metrics across banks.
  • Indian Steel Authority (SAIL) announces a 28‑park development plan under BHAVYA, allocating ₹33.66 crore to 100 plug‑and‑play industrial parks over the next six years (FY 2026‑31) – a key policy to streamline industrial growth and supply‑chain resilience, which has direct repercussions on the RBI’s capital‑adequacy relief to banks supporting manufacturing projects.
  • Visakhapatnam Steel Works (VSP) declares an ex‑gratia of ₹2 lakh per death and ₹50,000 per injury to families of the eight casualties that occurred on 08 Jun 2026 – a fact that retains memorability for exam‑readers focusing on industrial safety regulations and financial obligations of PSU steel units.
  • The Ministry of Finance extends the Credit Guarantee Scheme for Micro‑Finance Institutions‑2.0 (CGSMFI‑2.0) until 31 Aug 2026, raising the maximum loan cap to ₹1,000 crore – a regulatory move that will lift the guaranteed debt of MFIs from ₹770 crore to an anticipated ₹2.5 crore by year‑end, thereby easing liquidity strain in the frontier banking sector.
  • RBI’s 2026 “Payment Systems Survey” highlights that NEFT transfers reach 4.2 trillion ₹ per day with an average settlement time of 12 seconds” – a sign of scale that exam‑students should note as evidence of the digital‑payment ecosystem’s expansion ahead of the Digital Rupee launch.
  • National Bank for Agriculture and Rural Development (NABARD) reports that 8 crore loan books in the micro‑credit segment increased by 1.9 % to ₹548 crore on 30 Apr 2026 – emphasising the continuing emphasis on rural credit and the increasing share of NABARD loans in the RBI’s overall credit portfolio.

  • Prime Minister’s “12 Years of Garib Kalyan” message on 08 Jun 2026 cites six flagship schemes – Jan Dhan, Direct Benefit Transfer (DBT), Swachh Bharat, Pradhan Mantri Awas, Jal Jeevan Mission and Ayushman Bharat – each with a cumulative outlay of ₹5.35 lakh crore (inclusive of all related RTGS/NEFT transfer costs) – a study‑relevant recap of the new government’s welfare reach and fiscal commitment.
  • Population health scheme RN “Ayushman Bharat PM‑JAY” now covers 1.43 crore families in West Bengal (36th state/UT to join) – 1.24 crore beneficiaries, 527.58 crore rupees released FY 2026‑27 – a key metric used in exam questions on health‑indebtedness and micro‑insurance penetration.
  • PM Surakshit Matritva Abhiyan (PMSMA) marks its 10th‑year celebration on 09 Jun 2026 with a commemorative ₹75 coin and a ₹5 postal stamp, and introduces a “free anaesthetic and antenatal test (ANAT)” bundle to be offered on the 9th of each month – a detail that exam‑readers can recall when answering questions on maternal health spending.
  • Union Cabinet approves the “General Pool Residential Accommodation” (GPRA) for the Amaravati Special Economic Zone on 10 Jun 2026, costing ₹1.235 crore and providing 1,504 dwelling units, and awards the project “GRIHA 4‑Star” rating – a tangible example of centralized housing policy that increases investor confidence in SEZs.
  • Cabinet Committee on Economic Affairs releases a 10‑year medium‑term GDP forecast of 7.8 % for FY 2027‑28, citing the cumulative contribution of emerging sectors such as biotech, renewable energy, and digital services – a critical figure for exam‑students tackling macro‑economic growth questions.
  • The Ministry of Human Resources Development announces the “Pradhan Mantri Kisan Samman Nidhi” extension for FY 2026-27, rewarding 5.23 million farmers with ₹6,000 in conditional payments per crop cycle – a data point that reinforces the role of targeted cash transfers in rural development.

  • India signs the “India‑Switzerland Free Trade Agreement” (IFS‑FTA) on 15 May 2026, covering tariffs on 98 % of traded goods over 18 months, which unlocks ₹60 billion in cumulative preferential import duties – a key component of India’s trading profile often asked in exam questions on bilateral agreements.
  • India announces the signing of a Bilateral Investment Agreement (BIA) with Japan on 20 May 2026, covering sectors such as advanced robotics, green energy and digital infrastructure, valuing ₹1.5 trillion in investor protection mechanisms – an illustration of Indo‑Japan strategic coherence.

  • India advances “Digital India” diplomacy by partnering with the Atlantic Council to launch “It’s a Digital Village” summit on 15 June 2026 in Washington DC, focusing on 5G deployment and AI ethics – a modern policy move that highlights India’s diplomatic engagement in digital governance.

  • Indian export volume for FY 2025‑26 reached US$863 billion, comprising US$442 billion in merchandise and US$421 billion in services; most significant commodity share is petroleum products (30 % of merchandise exports) – a key statistic showing the country’s commodity trade exposure.
  • Port congestion data for 2025‑26: cargo handled rose to 915 MMT from 581 MMT in the previous year, while vessel turnaround time fell from 94 h to 48.8 h – a bullet that remains exam‑relevant when analysing logistics bottlenecks.
  • Coal Trade: The national import of 1.4 million tonne of coking coal during March–June 2026 allowed producers to reduce the deficit to 9 % as the export surplus of 3.2 million tonne of boilers remained unchanged – a detail exam‑students must internalise to answer questions on energy sector dynamics.
  • Western‑Asia crisis: Fertiliser stocks across India on 07 Jun 2026 show a surplus of 101.59 MT of urea (51 % of demand), commanding a price of ₹266 per bag; DAP prices remained ₹1,350 per bag – a data point often asked in MCQs on commodity pricing.
  • Digital Payments Index (DPI) – India’s payment‑performance weight is the highest (30 % of the index) with NEFT settled 10 trillion ₹ per day; UPI transactions hit 2,264 crore on 31 Mar 2026, valued at ₹29.53 lakh crore – exam‑students should note these figures when discussing digital‑payment momentum.
