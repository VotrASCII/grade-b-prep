Week 01 · General Awareness

01 Dec – 07 Dec, 2025

7 topics summarised · 80 practice questions

01

RBI & Monetary Policy

  • ★ TReDS limit – RBI reduced onboarding turnover limit for corporates/CPSEs to ₹ 250 crore (Notification 07‑Nov‑2024).
  • ★ Credit Guarantee Fund for MSEs – corpus increased by ₹ 9,000 crore; guarantee ceiling raised from ₹ 5 crore to ₹ 10 crore (effective 01‑Apr‑2025).
  • TReDS – part of RBI’s payment‑systems agenda; aims to improve trade‑receivable financing.
  • UCB tier classification – no new RBI circulars in this week.
  • Digital Rupee – no updates in the current batch.
02

Banking & Financial Sector

  • ★ SIM‑binding direction (DoT) – mandatory continuous SIM‑device binding for app‑based communication services; web‑session auto‑logout every 6 hrs; targets fraud loss ₹ 22,800 crore (2024).
  • Sanchar Saathi App – DoT direction dated 28‑Nov‑2025 requires pre‑installation on all handsets imported/manufactured for India; rollout in 90 days, compliance report in 120 days.
  • PMNRF ex‑gratia (Sivaganga mishap) – compensation ₹ 2 lakh per deceased kin, ₹ 50,000 per injured person (issued 01‑Dec‑2025).
  • Coal‑exploration approval revamp – removal of Jan‑2022 committee approval; 3‑month reduction in Geological Report approval time; private Accredited Prospecting Agencies (APAs) now prepare reports.
  • PESO digital licensing – National Single Window System (NSWS) for explosives licences; extended licence validity; mandatory electronic detonators and SETT (System for Explosives Tracking & Tracing).
  • Atal Pension Yojana enrolments – 8.34 crore total, women enrolments 4.04 crore (48 %) as of 31‑Oct‑2025.
  • TRAI (Wayanad, Oct 2025) – Call‑Setup Success Rate (CSSR) – Airtel 87.59 %, BSNL 86.98 %, Jio 92.19 %, Vi 92.29 %; Drop Call Rate (DCR) – Airtel 0.21 %, BSNL 5.20 %, Jio 1.01 %, Vi 1.22 %.
  • CGCA (Western Zone) – revenue ≈ ₹ 6,000 crore (Licence fee + spectrum) FY 2024‑25; pension outflow ≈ ₹ 5,000 crore.
  • Bank of Maharashtra OFS – Government divestment up to 6 % (5 % equity + 1 % green‑shoe) at ₹ 54 per share floor price; subscription 4.07× the issue.
  • RBI Guidelines on Regional Languages (02 Dec 2025) – PSBs to issue trilingual (Hindi‑English‑regional) material; recruitment of “Local Bank Officers”; Local‑Language Proficiency Test (LPT) for Customer Service Associates.
03

Government Schemes & Budget

  • Champion Sector – Footwear (Ministry of DPIIT, 01 Dec 2025) – footwear declared a “Champion Sector”; ecosystem and investment incentives announced.
  • Mission Shakti (Nari Shakti) – One‑Stop Centres, fast‑track courts, helplines and social‑security hubs for women (announced 01 Dec 2025).
  • Operation Sagar Bandhu – Sri Lanka – continued disaster‑relief assistance after Cyclone Ditwah (01 Dec 2025).
  • DHRUVA – Digital Hub for Reference & Unique Virtual Address – amendment to Post Office Act 2023 proposed; public comments due 31 Dec 2025.
  • Footwear Design & Development Institute (FDDI) Convocation – Institute of National Importance since 2017; > 500 graduates; emphasis on sustainable, biodegradable leather.
  • Rabi‑crop sowing update – total Rabi area 637.81 L ha; wheat sown 312.35 L ha (+ 16.91 %).
  • Winter Session 2025 – Parliamentary agenda – GST reforms cited as “citizen‑trust builder”.
  • Atal Bhujal Yojana (ABY) – pilot for participatory groundwater management; 3‑month faster GR approval.
  • Jal Jeevan Mission (JJM) – central outlay ₹ 2,08,652 crore; by 01 Dec 2025, 81.34 % of 19.36 crore rural households have tap water; extension to Dec 2028 announced in FY 2025‑26 Budget.
  • PM Kisan Yojana – total enrolments 8.34 crore (as of 31 Oct 2025).
  • Kali Sanskriti Vikas Yojana (KSVY) – grants for Gurus ₹ 15,000 / month, Shishyas ₹ 2,000‑10,000 / month, cultural organisations ₹ 1‑5 crore, etc.
  • Kashi‑Tamil Sangamam 4.0 – inaugurated 02 Dec 2025; 1,400 delegates; knowledge partners IIT Madras & BHU; 10 Union ministries support.
04

Economy & Trade

  • ★ Footwear sector – India ranks 2ⁿᵈ globally in production and consumption; FY 2024‑25 exports US $2,500 million, imports US $680 million (≈ 4 × export).
  • Coal‑exploration revamp – 3‑month reduction in Geological Report approval accelerates private‑sector participation.
  • Rabi‑crop area – wheat area up 16.91 %; overall Rabi sowing 393 L ha (+ 35.33 % vs 2024‑25).
  • International IDEA – CEC Gyanesh Kumar to assume chair on 3 Dec 2025; total electors across 35 member + 2 observer countries 2,225.55 million.
  • GST reforms – highlighted in Winter Session 2025 as building “trust among citizens”.
  • PMNRF ex‑gratia – ₹ 2 lakh per deceased, ₹ 50,000 per injured (Sivaganga mishap).
  • Footwear sustainability MoU – with University of Northampton; focus on circular‑economy, banana‑fiber biodegradable leather.
  • PM Kisan enrolments – women constitute 48 % (4.04 crore).
05

International Affairs & Organizations

  • ★ India chairing International IDEA in 2026; elected on 3 Dec 2025; total electors 2.23 billion.
  • Phone call – Sri Lanka President – PM Modi expressed condolences after Cyclone Ditwah; reaffirmed support under Operation Sagar Bandhu (01 Dec 2025).
  • Wishes to Begum Khaleda Zia (Bangladesh) – PM Modi offered “all possible support” (01 Dec 2025).
  • G20 presidency – not mentioned in this week’s releases.
  • Bilateral FTAs – no new signings reported; India‑UK CETA signed 24 Jul 2025 (not in this batch).
06

Awards, Rankings & Appointments

  • President Droupadi Murmu – presided over FDDI convocation (01 Dec 2025).
  • CEC Gyanesh Kumar – to chair International IDEA (effective 3 Dec 2025).
  • Chief Controller of Explosives R. N. Meena – highlighted PESO as facilitator; stressed digital licensing & safety‑by‑design.
  • Rajya Sabha Chairman C. P. Radhakrishnan – felicitation; noted reforms of Coir Board and multiple gubernatorial stints.
07

Sports / Environment / Science & Tech

  • Siddha Varmam therapy (in‑flight) – medical aid using traditional Varmam technique on a flight (01 Dec 2025).
  • PESO safety initiatives – digital licences (NSWS), SETT, electronic detonators across petroleum, gas, hydrogen & explosives chains.
  • Footwear sustainability – MoU with University of Northampton for circular‑economy materials (banana‑fiber leather).
  • DHRUVA digital addressing – “Address‑as‑a‑Service” platform; virtual address identifiers; public comments due 31 Dec 2025.
  • SIM‑binding for apps – extends banking‑style security to messaging apps; curtails phishing, digital‑arrest and impersonation scams.
